Results Commentary
Marvell's Q4 FY2026 confirmed the AI custom silicon ramp is accelerating. AI revenue of $1.1B grew +64% sequentially — the fastest quarterly sequential growth in company history — driven by Amazon Trainium/Inferentia ASIC volume deployments and optical DSP ramp. Full-year FY2026 revenue of $8.2B (+42% YoY) exceeded the company's own guidance. The explicit FY2027 guide of ~$11B was the catalyst that sent analyst targets well above the stock price.
We are in the early stages of a massive AI infrastructure build-out. Our custom silicon and electro-optics platforms position Marvell uniquely for the next wave of hyperscale expansion.
— Matt Murphy, CEO, Q4 FY2026 Earnings Call

Key Metrics Analysis
The AI revenue of $1.1B (+22% vs $900M estimate) was the defining beat — it puts AI on a $4B+ quarterly run-rate heading into FY2027. Non-GAAP gross margins of 60.2% (+70bps) reflect the premium pricing of custom ASIC products. The GAAP loss improved to -$0.24 (vs -$0.28 expected), showing the amortization drag is slowly declining. CEO Murphy's open-market stock purchases before earnings added credibility to the guidance.
