Revenue Analysis
MercadoLibre's revenue grew 4x in four years — driven by three compounding growth engines: (1) e-commerce marketplace (GMV +29% FX-neutral Q4 2025), (2) Mercado Pago fintech (78M MAU, $12.5B credit portfolio +90% YoY), and (3) advertising (+67% FX-neutral). Latin America has 650M people with ~15% e-commerce penetration vs 30%+ in the US — the secular growth runway is measured in decades. The JPMorgan downgrade and Shopee competition have driven the stock -30% from highs, creating a potential entry point into one of the world's best internet businesses.

Net Income Analysis
MercadoLibre's earnings power is real — $2B+ GAAP net income — but margin compression is the key investor concern. Management has 'embraced higher investment spending' (JPMorgan's description) to counter Shopee's Brazilian market entry. The fundamental question: is this temporary investment spending (bullish) or structural competitive erosion (bearish)? The credit portfolio's 90% growth and advertising's 67% growth suggest genuine business momentum despite the margin pressure.

Debt Analysis
MercadoLibre's corporate debt of $8B is manageable at 1.3x EBITDA. The $12.5B credit portfolio is separate — this is the Mercado Credito lending book, funded by consumer deposits and debt, generating net interest income. NPL rates on the credit portfolio are the key risk in a Latin American economic downturn. Corporate balance sheet risk is moderate; fintech credit risk is the more nuanced concern.

Is MercadoLibre, Inc. Financially Strong or Weakening? — Definitive Assessment
MercadoLibre is one of the world's great internet businesses — a genuine Amazon + PayPal + Shopify of Latin America that is growing revenue 39% annually with 650M potential customers and sub-20% e-commerce penetration. The stock is down 30%+ from highs due to Shopee competition concerns and margin pressure. At 28x forward earnings — among the cheapest multiples in MELI's history — the risk/reward is compelling for patient long-term investors who believe the structural Latin American growth story is intact. Financial health: A. Trend: Margin recovery is the key catalyst. Strong Buy for 3-5 year horizon.
