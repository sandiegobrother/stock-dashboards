#1
Continued Gross Margin Negativity
Financial
CRITICAL
10/10
65%
Eos's gross margin was approximately -30% in FY2025 — meaning every dollar of revenue costs $1.30 to produce. Until the zinc-air manufacturing process achieves scale economics, every unit shipped deepens cash losses. The FY2026 guidance of $300-400M revenue requires not just volume growth but a dramatic gross margin improvement to positive territory simultaneously.
Mitigant: Management has identified specific manufacturing process improvements (automation, yield optimization) that they project will drive gross margin toward positive. The zinc chemistry is fundamentally lower-cost than lithium at scale.
#2
FY2026 Revenue Guidance History of Misses
Financial
CRITICAL
9/10
60%
Eos has missed its aggressive revenue guidance in multiple prior years — creating significant credibility deficit with institutional investors. The FY2025 $114M revenue represented only a fraction of earlier guidance. The FY2026 $300-400M guide requires 163-250% growth, which would be the third consecutive guidance-beating year (FY2025 was +7x) but from a very low base.
Mitigant: FY2025 +7x revenue growth demonstrates the manufacturing ramp is real. $624M in cash from $600M senior notes provides runway regardless of near-term guidance outcomes.
#3
$600M Senior Notes Debt Burden
Financial
HIGH
8/10
65%
Eos raised $600M in senior notes to fund operations — this debt accrues interest (estimated 10-12% PIK) that compounds the cash consumption rate. If the FY2026 revenue and margin targets are missed, the debt balance grows faster than the business, accelerating toward a potential restructuring in FY2027-2028.
Mitigant: $624M cash balance provides 2+ years of operational runway at current burn rates. If positive gross margins are achieved in FY2026, FCF break-even becomes visible.
#4
Tesla Megapack and CATL Competition
Competition
HIGH
8/10
70%
Tesla Megapack (lithium iron phosphate) and CATL (dominant Chinese LFP supplier) already have proven, reliable products at competitive prices. Grid operators and utilities are risk-averse — they prefer proven technology over unproven zinc-air, even if zinc offers theoretical long-duration advantages.
Mitigant: Eos targets 8-12 hour storage (vs Tesla Megapack's 2-4 hours) — a differentiated use case where zinc chemistry's long-duration economics are genuinely superior.
#5
IRA Tax Credit Dependency
Regulatory
HIGH
7/10
30%
Eos's economic model depends significantly on IRA domestic manufacturing tax credits for US-made energy storage. Any rollback or reinterpretation of IRA energy storage credits under a new administration would directly impair project economics for Eos's customers, reducing demand.
Mitigant: Eos's US manufacturing (100% made in America) qualifies for the most favorable IRA credit tiers. Political support for US manufacturing creates bipartisan protection.
#6
Going Concern History
Financial
HIGH
7/10
25%
Eos received a going concern qualification from auditors in prior years — a rare designation that signals existential financial risk. While the $624M capital raise resolved the immediate concern, the history creates persistent investor skepticism.
Mitigant: $624M cash explicitly resolved going concern. Auditors removed the qualification following the capital raise.
