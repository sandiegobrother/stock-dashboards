Volatility Decay / Beta Slippage
The single biggest risk of SMCL is NOT SMCI's direction — it's the mathematics of daily leveraged compounding. In a volatile, range-bound SMCI, SMCL will lose money even if SMCI is flat. This effect accelerates with SMCI's high beta. Only suitable for 1-5 day holds around clear directional catalysts.
HIGH
SMCI Gross Margin Decline
10 consecutive quarters of gross margin decline. If Q3 FY2026 does NOT show recovery, SMCI will likely re-test $25-28 lows — SMCL amplifies to ~$5-8. This is the most actionable fundamental risk.
HIGH
Revenue Guidance Miss
SMCI missed its Q1 FY2026 guidance by over $1B. The $40B FY2026 guide requires $15B+ quarters in H2. Any guidance reduction = -20-30% SMCI → -40-60% SMCL.
MEDIUM
DOJ / Accounting Regulatory Risk
SMCI filed late 10-K in 2024 and faced DOJ scrutiny over accounting practices. While resolved for now, any recurrence of accounting irregularities would be catastrophic and 2x amplified via SMCL.
MEDIUM
Customer Concentration
Top 5 hyperscaler customers represent ~70% of revenue. Loss or deferral by any single customer creates significant revenue risk. Microsoft, Meta, Google, Amazon are likely among top clients — they wield enormous negotiating power.

GraniteShares has closed small AUM ETFs in the past. If SMCL AUM falls below ~$20M, GraniteShares could liquidate the fund. Current AUM ~$50-100M provides reasonable buffer.
Risk Verdict · SMCL · Very High Risk · Short-Term Tactical Only

SMCL carries some of the highest risk of any ETF in this platform. The 2x daily leverage means a single bad SMCI earnings print can result in a 30-50% single-day SMCL loss. Combined with SMCI's own elevated risk profile (margin compression, customer concentration, accounting history), SMCL is appropriate ONLY as a short-duration tactical trade around specific catalysts — primarily the Q3 FY2026 earnings on May 5, 2026. Maximum recommended holding period: 1-5 trading days. Never hold through earnings without a defined stop-loss.
