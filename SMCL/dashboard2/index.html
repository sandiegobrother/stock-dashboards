Margin Compression — The Critical Issue
Gross Margin Trend (%)
Why Margins Are Collapsing

SMCI's gross margin has fallen from 18% → 6.3% in 3 years — one of the most severe margin compressions in large-cap tech.

Cause 1 — Hyperscaler pricing power: Amazon, Google, Microsoft, and Meta are SMCI's largest customers and negotiate aggressively on server pricing. As AI capex scales, they extract lower per-unit costs.

Cause 2 — Component cost mix: GPU-dense AI servers have lower software/service content, inherently carrying lower margins than traditional rack servers.

Cause 3 — Competition: Dell's PowerEdge servers are gaining AI traction, putting a ceiling on SMCI's pricing power. Nvidia's own direct infrastructure offerings add further pressure.

Q3 FY2026 guide implies margin recovery — management guided for "anticipated improvements." This is the single most important metric to watch on May 5, 2026.

SMCL ETF Mechanics
Daily Leverage Reset

SMCL rebalances daily to maintain 2x exposure. Over multiple days, the compounding of 2x daily returns produces a cumulative return that diverges from 2x SMCI's period return — especially in volatile conditions. Example: SMCI -10% then +10% = -1% net. SMCL: -20% then +20% = -4% net — 4x worse.

Expense Ratio

SMCL charges approximately 1.15% annual expense ratio — high vs traditional ETFs but typical for single-stock leveraged products. The cost drag compounds over time, adding to the volatility decay effect for longer holding periods.

Financial Assessment · SMCI / SMCL · High Growth, Compressed Margins

SMCI is a high-revenue-growth, low-margin AI infrastructure play. The $40B+ FY2026 guide represents 73% revenue growth — exceptional. But 6.3% gross margin means net income of only ~$1.5B on $40B revenue (3.75% net margin). The bull case requires margins recovering to 8-10%+ as liquid cooling becomes a more premium product. SMCL amplifies both the revenue growth tailwind and the margin compression headwind by 2x daily.
