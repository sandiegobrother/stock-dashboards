Results Commentary
NVIDIA delivered its 9th consecutive earnings beat, with Data Center revenue of $62.0B growing +75% YoY — representing 91% of total revenue. The $78B Q1 FY2027 guidance was the most important number: it came in $6B above the $72B street consensus, confirming that Blackwell demand has not peaked. GAAP net income of ~$43B was up 94% YoY. Non-GAAP gross margins of 74.6% held near record levels despite the complexity of ramping the new Blackwell architecture. Gaming was the only weak segment (-11% YoY) due to supply constraints redirecting capacity to Data Center.
Computing demand is growing exponentially — the agentic AI inflection point has arrived.
— Jensen Huang, CEO, Q4 FY2026 Earnings Call

Key Metrics Analysis
The Data Center +7.5% revenue beat vs estimates was the key upside driver. Blackwell GPU shipments accelerated significantly in Q4, with NVL72 rack systems generating revenue at $3M+ per rack. Professional Visualization's +159% YoY growth reflects AI workstation demand from enterprise and creative markets. Automotive +103% reflects the DRIVE AGX platform ramp at automotive OEMs. The only miss was Gaming at $2.7B vs $2.9B expected — supply is being prioritized for Data Center, creating a temporary Gaming shortfall that is not structurally concerning.

Guidance Analysis
The Q1 FY2027 guide of $78B vs the $72B consensus is the single most important data point from this earnings report. An $6B upside guidance surprise from a $68B revenue base implies sequential growth acceleration — contrary to bears who expected Blackwell to plateau. Jensen Huang's comment about 'agentic AI inflection' suggests demand broadening beyond hyperscalers to enterprise and sovereign AI customers. The $78B guide also excluded any potential revenue from China, meaning any export policy relaxation would be purely incremental.
