#1
Natural Gas Price Volatility
Economic
HIGH
8/10
55%
Bloom Energy's fuel cells run primarily on natural gas — a significant long-term strategic tension for a 'clean energy' company. Natural gas price spikes (as in 2022: +130% YoY) directly impair the economics of Bloom Energy systems for customers and reduce new system orders. Additionally, natural gas combustion is incompatible with corporate net-zero commitments.
Mitigant: Bloom is transitioning customers toward hydrogen and biogas operation. The solid oxide fuel cell platform is fuel-agnostic — the same hardware can run on hydrogen when available.
#2
GAAP Profitability Timeline Uncertainty
Financial
HIGH
8/10
55%
Bloom has been promising GAAP profitability 'soon' for multiple years — each guidance update pushes the timeline further. The stock has de-rated from $28 (2021) to $18 (2026) partly due to this credibility gap. FY2025 FCF positive for the first time is promising, but GAAP profitability requires further gross margin expansion.
Mitigant: FY2025 first-ever positive FCF is the most credible profitability signal yet. Gross margin improvement from 12% to 32% demonstrates the cost trajectory is real.
#3
IRA Tax Credit Rollback Risk
Regulatory
HIGH
8/10
35%
Bloom Energy's project economics and new order pipeline are substantially supported by IRA hydrogen production credits (45V) and investment tax credits (48C). Any rollback of IRA clean energy provisions — actively debated in Congress — would reduce the economic attractiveness of Bloom Energy systems for customers.
Mitigant: Bloom's 100% US manufacturing qualifies it for the highest domestic content credit tiers. Republican support for 'energy security' (even natural gas) partially protects against clean energy-specific rollbacks.
#4
Hyperscale AI Data Center Customer Risk
Competition
HIGH
7/10
35%
6 hyperscale AI data center customers represent the primary near-term growth driver — but these relationships are early-stage and not yet under long-term contracts in all cases. If hyperscalers find alternative power solutions (small modular reactors, grid connections, other distributed generation), Bloom's data center revenue growth could disappoint.
Mitigant: Grid interconnect timelines of 6-18 months structurally require on-site generation for data centers built today. Bloom's SOFC advantage (natural gas + hydrogen capable) addresses specific data center reliability requirements.
#5
Hydrogen Economy Timeline Uncertainty
Industry Disruption
MEDIUM
6/10
55%
Bloom's long-term bull case depends on green hydrogen becoming economically viable — reducing the natural gas dependency and enabling true zero-carbon operation. Green hydrogen cost curves have improved but remain 3-5x natural gas equivalent pricing. The hydrogen economy may take longer than projected to develop.
Mitigant: Bloom Energy systems work on existing natural gas infrastructure today — they don't require hydrogen infrastructure to be deployed and generate revenue currently.
#6
Competition from Battery Storage Systems
Competition
MEDIUM
5/10
45%
Tesla Megapack and battery energy storage systems (BESS) compete for the same data center backup power budget. Battery costs have declined dramatically — LFP batteries are now below $100/kWh, reducing the cost gap with fuel cells for certain applications.
Mitigant: Bloom Energy provides both baseload power generation and backup — batteries are backup-only. The use cases are partially complementary rather than purely competitive.

Risk Verdict
Risk Summary · BE · MODERATE RISK · 0 Critical · 4 High Risk Factors
Bloom Energy's risk profile is elevated — natural gas dependency creates a strategic tension with clean energy positioning, and IRA rollback is a genuine regulatory risk. However, the 6 hyperscale AI customers and gross margin recovery to 32% provide near-term revenue anchors. Most dangerous risk: IRA tax credit rollback coinciding with natural gas price spike — double compression of project economics and fuel costs making Bloom Energy systems uncompetitive against alternatives.
