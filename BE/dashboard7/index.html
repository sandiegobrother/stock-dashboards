Why Institutions Buy

AI Data Center Power Crisis — 6 Hyperscale Customers Locked In
AI CATALYST
Bloom Energy is one of the few companies with a proven solution to the AI data center power crisis — on-site solid oxide fuel cells deployable in 6-9 months vs 18+ months for grid connection. Six hyperscale AI customers have validated Bloom's solution for this specific use case. The AI data center power demand is structural and multi-year, creating the most favorable market environment Bloom has ever experienced.

For clean energy investors who have waited for Bloom to prove its economics, the 32% GM is the validation they needed.

Why Institutions Avoid
A1
Natural Gas Dependency Conflicts with ESG Mandates
ESG
Bloom Energy's fuel cells primarily run on natural gas — structurally incompatible with net-zero commitments and ESG-mandated portfolios that require zero-carbon energy. The 'clean energy' branding creates an institutional credibility gap: ESG-focused clean energy funds often cannot hold Bloom because natural gas combustion generates CO2 regardless of efficiency improvements.
A2
IRA Tax Credit Rollback Risk
REGULATORY
The 45X manufacturing credit and 48C investment credit that underpin Bloom's customer economics are subject to rollback in Congressional budget negotiations. Energy-sector institutional investors who run regulatory sensitivity models assign 20-30% probability to IRA credit reduction that would impair Bloom's competitive pricing by $0.03-0.05/kWh.
