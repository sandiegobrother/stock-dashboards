Revenue Analysis
Carvana's revenue trajectory tells the story of a near-death experience and remarkable recovery. 2022 was the crisis year — used car prices crashed, the company was overleveraged, the stock fell 98%. The 2023 restructuring stabilized the business, and 2024-2025 saw record performance. 596K units in FY2025 (+43% YoY), record Q3 2025 revenue of $5.65B (+55% YoY). The Q3 2025 net income of $263M and EBITDA of $637M (11.3% margin) validated the operational model at scale.

Debt Analysis
Carvana's $5.4B debt remains the central financial risk. The PIK period (interest added to principal) converted to cash requirements in 2026 — adding ~$400-500M in annual cash interest. At 2.1x ND/EBITDA and declining, the trajectory is positive. The debt is manageable if the business continues executing at Q3 2025 levels — but leaves no room for operational setbacks.
