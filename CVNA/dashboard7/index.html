Why Institutions Buy
B1
From Near-Bankruptcy to $2.23B EBITDA — Most Dramatic Turnaround in Retail
TURNAROUND
Carvana went from near-bankruptcy in 2022 ($10/share) to $2.23B adj. EBITDA and 11.3% EBITDA margins in FY2025. The turnaround speed and magnitude is one of the most impressive in retail history. Institutional investors who participated in the recovery made extraordinary returns. The question for today's buyers: is the turnaround fully priced at $361, or is there a second act?
B2
1.2% Used Car Market Share in $1T Market — Decades of Runway
RUNWAY
Carvana has 1.2% market share in the $1T annual US used car market — so small that doubling share (to 2.4%) represents $12B in incremental revenue. The AI-powered operations model (reconditioning, pricing, logistics) gets more efficient at scale. CarMax at 4% share shows the ceiling exists and is achievable. Institutional growth investors see 10-20 years of market share capture ahead.
B3
AI Operations — Industry-Leading 11.3% EBITDA Margin vs CarMax 4%
COMPETITIVE MOAT
Carvana's EBITDA margin of 11.3% vs CarMax's 4% reflects a fundamental AI operations advantage — automated reconditioning, dynamic pricing, and AI-powered logistics create structural cost advantages. This margin differential widens at scale and is why institutional investors believe Carvana will continue gaining share: better margin economics at lower prices for consumers.
Why Institutions Avoid
A1
$5.4B Debt + PIK-to-Cash Conversion 2026
LEVERAGE
The PIK-to-cash interest conversion adds $400-500M in annual cash obligations in 2026 — the defining financial test of the turnaround sustainability. High-yield debt investors and credit-focused hedge funds scrutinize CVNA's FCF coverage of this new cash interest requirement. Any GPU compression or volume miss reduces coverage ratio rapidly.
A2
Garcia Family Governance — Related-Party Risk
GOVERNANCE
The Garcia family's DriveTime relationship and controlling position create governance concerns that ESG-mandated and governance-focused institutions cannot ignore. Proxy advisory firms have flagged related-party transaction risk. Governance-first institutional mandates maintain zero-weight positions in CVNA regardless of fundamental quality.

Investment Thesis
Long-Term Investment Thesis · CVNA · Portfolio Manager Perspective
Carvana's investment thesis is about the long-term disruption of traditional auto retail by an AI-powered digital platform. The turnaround from near-bankruptcy proves the model is financially viable — 11.3% EBITDA margins at 596K units is a superior economics profile vs any traditional dealer. The $1T used car market with 1.2% share provides genuine 10-15 year growth runway. The PIK-to-cash conversion in 2026 is the near-term test: if $1.8B FCF covers $400-500M in new cash interest with room for growth investment, the turnaround is permanently validated. The governance concerns are real but manageable for investors focused on the fundamental business quality.
