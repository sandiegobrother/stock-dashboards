Debt Analysis
Strategy's $8.2B debt must be evaluated relative to BTC holdings, not EBITDA. The LTV ratio of 16% ($8.2B debt / $51.7B BTC value at $70K) is conservative by real estate standards and significantly below any stress-test threshold. None of the convertible notes mature until 2028, giving years of runway. The preferred stock instruments (STRC, STRK, STRD, STRF) add ~$3-4B in additional capital obligations but also raise capital for further BTC purchases. Cantor Fitzgerald's analysis confirms: Bitcoin would need to fall to ~$7,600 (~90% decline) to trigger forced selling — not a plausible scenario.

Strategy's ROE and ROIC cannot be evaluated using traditional frameworks. Is Strategy Inc. (formerly MicroStrategy) Financially Strong or Weakening? — Definitive Assessment
Strategy is the most unconventional company in this portfolio — it is explicitly not a technology company, software company, or traditional equity in any meaningful sense. It is a leveraged Bitcoin treasury vehicle. Traditional financial analysis yields near-zero insight. The software business is profitable but irrelevant to valuation. The correct analysis framework: (1) Is the 16% BTC LTV debt ratio acceptable? (Yes — Cantor confirms no forced selling risk.) (2) Will Bitcoin appreciate from $70K? (Bull case: yes significantly. Bear case: no.) (3) Is the premium-to-NAV multiple sustainable? This depends entirely on Bitcoin sentiment. At $34% upside to consensus with a $705 bull case, MSTR offers extreme asymmetry — in both directions. Financial health grade: C+ (traditional metrics inapplicable). This is a leveraged Bitcoin position, not an equity investment. Appropriate only for Bitcoin conviction holders with high risk tolerance.
