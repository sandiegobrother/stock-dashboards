Why Institutions Buy
B1
84% EBITDA Margin — Best in Global Ad Technology
QUALITY
AppLovin's 84% EBITDA margins are the highest of any scaled advertising technology company globally. By comparison: Meta has 52%, Alphabet 35%, The Trade Desk 35%. This margin structure reflects Axon AI's software economics — the engine improves with every ad auction without proportional cost increases. Institutional investors running software quality screens rank AppLovin's margin profile as best-in-class.
B2
E-Commerce Expansion — $1.45B 2026 Target = New Revenue Vertical
GROWTH
Applying Axon's mobile optimization engine to e-commerce and DTC advertising opens a $150B+ addressable market that AppLovin hasn't previously accessed. Early pilots demonstrating superior ROAS vs Meta and Google for certain product categories are the most important data points for institutional growth investors. If $1.45B is achieved in 2026, it validates the 'Axon is generalizable' thesis worth $20B+ in incremental market cap.
B3
$6.7B in Buybacks — Management Allocating Capital Confidently
CAPITAL RETURN
AppLovin has repurchased $6.7B in shares over 3 years at prices that have since appreciated significantly. Management buying back stock at 35x P/E signals genuine conviction that intrinsic value significantly exceeds the current share price. For institutional investors who track insider and management capital allocation signals, AppLovin's buyback program at scale is a meaningful positive.
Why Institutions Avoid
A1
SEC Investigation — Unknown Scope, Binary Risk
REGULATORY
The disclosed SEC investigation creates binary risk that value and risk-parity managers cannot model. Unknown scope means unknown liability — investigations can close with no action (most likely) or result in material fines, restatements, or executive actions that impair the investment case. Governance-sensitive institutions maintain reduced positions until clarity emerges.
A2
3.5% Short Interest — Elevated vs Large-Cap Ad Tech
SENTIMENT
AppLovin's 3.5% short interest is elevated relative to ad tech peers (Meta 0.9%, Alphabet 0.6%) — reflecting both the SEC uncertainty and persistent skepticism about the e-commerce expansion claims. Two separate short-seller reports in 2025 alleged click fraud and inflated performance metrics. While the company disputed these, the short interest creates a persistent multiple overhang.

Investment Thesis
Long-Term Investment Thesis · APP · Portfolio Manager Perspective
AppLovin is a rare combination of exceptional business quality (84% EBITDA margins) and a clearly identifiable near-term re-rating catalyst (SEC resolution). The Axon AI engine's demonstrated superiority in mobile advertising creates a compounding revenue flywheel — every advertiser who sees better ROAS on AppLovin rebalances more budget to the platform. The e-commerce expansion is the growth optionality that institutional growth investors are buying — a $150B addressable market for a proven AI optimization engine. The SEC investigation is the near-term valuation constraint: its resolution (most likely with no material action) would remove the primary institutional avoidance reason. For a fund that has done the diligence on the advertising technology, AppLovin at 35x P/E for 84% EBITDA margins and $4B FCF growing at 25%+ is genuinely attractive.
