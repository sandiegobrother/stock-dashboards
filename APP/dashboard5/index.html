#1
SEC Investigation — Scope Unknown
Regulatory
CRITICAL
9/10
45%
AppLovin disclosed an SEC investigation in early 2025 — the specific scope is unknown, but potential areas include revenue recognition, related-party transactions, and advertising performance claims. An adverse SEC enforcement action could result in fines, management changes, and a severe multiple compression from the already-elevated 35x P/E.
Mitigant: AppLovin's 84% EBITDA margins and clean FCF generation suggest revenue recognition manipulation would be difficult to construct. Management has not disclosed material accounting concerns.
#2
AI Advertising Disruption Risk
Industry Disruption
HIGH
8/10
40%
AI-powered ad targeting tools (Google's Performance Max, Meta's Advantage+) directly compete with AppLovin's Axon AI engine. If Google or Meta develop mobile game advertising capabilities equivalent to AppLovin's at lower cost, the 84% EBITDA margin premium would compress. AppLovin's e-commerce expansion is competing directly against Google and Meta's core franchises.
Mitigant: AppLovin's proprietary mobile gaming data (billions of events/day) and SDK distribution (300M+ devices) create a data flywheel that Google and Meta cannot easily replicate in-app.
#3
Customer Concentration — Top Advertisers
Economic
HIGH
7/10
35%
AppLovin's mobile advertising revenue is concentrated among a relatively small number of large mobile game publishers and DTC e-commerce advertisers. The loss of 2-3 top advertisers, or an industry-wide mobile game monetization downturn, could impair the revenue trajectory materially.
Mitigant: Axon 2.0 consistently demonstrates measurable ROAS improvements — advertisers have strong financial incentives to remain on the platform regardless of alternative options.
#4
E-Commerce Expansion Execution Risk
Industry Disruption
HIGH
7/10
45%
AppLovin's e-commerce advertising expansion ($1.45B target for 2026) requires winning budgets from advertisers who have established Meta and Google relationships. Demonstrating superior ROAS in e-commerce vs mobile gaming (where AppLovin's data advantage is strongest) is not yet proven at scale.
Mitigant: Early e-commerce pilots showed strong ROAS results. The Axon engine's optimization capabilities have been demonstrated to be generalizable across verticals.
#5
Mobile Gaming Secular Decline
Industry Disruption
MEDIUM
6/10
40%
Mobile gaming revenue growth has slowed post-COVID as consumers returned to other entertainment options. If mobile gaming as an advertising category declines, AppLovin's core revenue base contracts even if e-commerce expansion succeeds.
Mitigant: Mobile gaming remains the largest digital advertising category globally. Casual gaming apps (puzzle, match-3) have shown resilient engagement regardless of economic cycles.
#6
Short Seller Attacks
Financial
MEDIUM
5/10

Two separate short-seller reports in 2025 alleged click fraud and inflated performance metrics. While AppLovin disputed these claims, the reports temporarily suppressed the stock and created ongoing credibility questions that the SEC investigation perpetuates.
Mitigant: AppLovin's financials are audited by a Big Four firm. The 84% EBITDA margin and strong FCF are verifiable through cash flows, making revenue manipulation structurally difficult.

Risk Verdict
Risk Summary · APP · ELEVATED RISK · 1 Critical · 3 High Risk Factors
AppLovin's risk profile is elevated due to the SEC investigation creating an unresolvable uncertainty overhang. The underlying business quality (84% EBITDA margins, $4B FCF, $6.7B in buybacks) is exceptional. The SEC investigation is the binary risk — resolved favorably → stock re-rates significantly higher; adverse enforcement → severe multiple compression. Most dangerous risk: SEC enforcement action combined with e-commerce expansion failing to offset any mobile gaming deceleration.
