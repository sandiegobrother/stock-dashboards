Revenue Analysis
CAVA's revenue growth story is the restaurant industry equivalent of early Chipotle — consistent 20-30%+ annual growth driven by unit expansion (adding 70+ net new restaurants per year) and strong same-restaurant sales. FY2025 was notable for the SSS story: CAVA delivered +0.5% SSS vs the -1.1% estimate — a 160 basis point beat that triggered a 25% stock surge. This confirmed brand resilience against macro headwinds. The unit economics are exceptional: average unit volume (AUV) exceeds $2.9M, restaurant-level margins are 21-25%, and new unit productivity exceeds 100% of system average. The path to 1,000+ restaurants (from 439 today) represents a decade of visible unit growth.

CAVA achieved first-ever GAAP profitability in 2024 — a major milestone for a fast-casual growth company. Free Cash Flow Analysis
CAVA's FCF inflection from negative to positive in 2024 ($53M) is the hallmark of a fast-casual concept reaching maturity. FY2025 FCF was slightly lower as the company invested in new kitchen equipment for the Salmon menu launch and accelerated unit openings. This is normal and expected — CapEx for new restaurant openings temporarily compresses FCF at this stage. As CAVA reaches its 500-600 restaurant scale, the growing cash generation from the existing fleet will begin to significantly exceed new unit CapEx requirements — the free cash flow flywheel will accelerate materially.

Margin Analysis
CAVA's gross margin has been remarkably consistent at 25% — reflecting the stable unit economics of Mediterranean fast-casual. The key margin expansion story is the adj. EBITDA margin: from -5% (2022) to +15% (2025) as revenue scale leveraged fixed G&A costs. Restaurant-level profit margin (RLP) has been 21-25%, with the best-performing class of 2024 openings exceeding 25%. The path from 15% adj. EBITDA margin to Chipotle's 28% margin is the long-term value creation opportunity.

CAVA has maintained a pristine zero-debt balance sheet from founding through 439 restaurants.
