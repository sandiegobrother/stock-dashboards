#1
Labor Cost Inflation — California/Minimum Wage
Economic
HIGH
8/10
70%
California's fast food minimum wage increased to $20/hour in 2024 and other states are following — restaurant labor is 25-30% of CAVA's cost structure. Each $1/hour wage increase across CAVA's workforce adds ~$15-20M annually to labor costs, directly compressing restaurant-level margins from the current 25%.
Mitigant: CAVA's Mediterranean menu (higher AUV, premium positioning) provides more pricing power than value-focused competitors. Menu price increases of 4-7% annually have been well-received.
#2
Valuation at 60x P/E — No Margin for Error
Financial
HIGH
8/10
55%
At 60x forward P/E, any same-store sales deceleration below +8%, unit opening miss, or margin compression would trigger severe multiple compression. CAVA has only 439 restaurants — execution missteps at this early scale can have outsized impact on reported metrics. The Chipotle comparison requires matching Chipotle's execution consistency, which took years to achieve.
Mitigant: CAVA's +10.8% same-store sales is accelerating, not decelerating — the current execution is validating the premium multiple.
#3
New Market Expansion Execution Risk
Industry Disruption
HIGH
7/10
40%
CAVA's growth requires opening 80-90+ new restaurants annually in markets outside its core East Coast base. New markets (Midwest, Mountain West) have different customer familiarity with Mediterranean cuisine — AUVs in new markets are typically 15-20% below mature market averages for the first 1-2 years.
Mitigant: CAVA conducts extensive market research before entering new cities. Catering and digital ordering have been proven as tools to build brand awareness ahead of restaurant openings.
#4
Food Cost Inflation — Olive Oil, Lamb, Feta
Economic
HIGH
7/10
55%
CAVA's Mediterranean menu relies on premium ingredients (feta cheese, lamb, olive oil, tahini) that have experienced significant commodity price inflation. Olive oil prices increased 100%+ in 2023-2024 due to Mediterranean drought. Sustained ingredient inflation could compress the 25% restaurant-level margin target.
Mitigant: CAVA locks in ingredient prices through multi-year supply contracts where possible. Premium positioning allows selective menu price increases to offset cost inflation.
#5
Competition from Sweetgreen, Chipotle, and Local Mediterranean Chains
Competition
MEDIUM
6/10
40%
Chipotle's test of Mediterranean bowls and Sweetgreen's protein bowl expansion both target CAVA's healthy fast casual positioning. Panera and emerging Mediterranean chains (Naya, The Halal Guys) compete at lower price points.
Mitigant: CAVA's authentic Mediterranean brand, community-sourced recipes, and vertically integrated dips/spreads distribution create differentiation that is difficult to quickly replicate.
#6
Catering Business Execution
Industry Disruption
LOW
4/10

CAVA is expanding catering as a margin-accretive revenue stream — but catering requires different operational capabilities (packaging, reliability, volume management) than restaurant service.
Mitigant: Catering is already contributing to AUV growth at mature CAVA locations. The operational infrastructure investment is modest compared to restaurant CapEx.

Risk Verdict
Risk Summary · CAVA · MODERATE RISK · 0 Critical · 4 High Risk Factors
CAVA's risk profile is moderate — the primary risk is the 60x P/E leaving no room for execution mistakes at a company with only 439 restaurants. Labor and food cost inflation are ongoing headwinds to the margin expansion story. Most dangerous risk: Labor cost inflation driving restaurant-level margins below 20% at the same time same-store sales decelerate below 7%, triggering multiple compression from 60x toward sector average 40x.
