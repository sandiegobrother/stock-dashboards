1 · Brand Strength
Brand Assessment · Score 7/10
7
SanDisk is one of the most recognized storage brands in consumer and enterprise — 30+ years of brand building across flash drives, memory cards, and enterprise SSDs. As an independent pure-play NAND company, Sandisk can focus its brand and R&D entirely on flash storage, freed from the HDD drag that diluted the Western Digital brand for years.

3 · Switching Costs
Switching Costs · Score 5/10
5
Enterprise SSD switching costs are moderate — IT teams that have qualified SanDisk SSDs in their servers face re-qualification costs, compatibility testing, and procurement disruption to switch. For hyperscalers running millions of SanDisk drives, switching vendors requires qualifying an alternative at scale while maintaining service continuity — typically 12-18 months and $5-20M.

4 · Cost Advantage
Cost Advantage · Score 6/10
6
The Kioxia JV provides ~20% of global NAND capacity — manufacturing scale that gives Sandisk cost parity with Samsung and SK Hynix without requiring sole ownership of all fab costs. As a pure-play NAND company, Sandisk can allocate all R&D to flash innovation rather than dividing attention between HDD and NAND as Western Digital did.

5 · Patents & Proprietary Technology
IP & Proprietary Tech · Score 7/10
7
Sandisk holds significant NAND cell design IP (from 30+ years of flash development), controller architecture patents, and storage system software IP. The 3D NAND architecture developed jointly with Kioxia represents layers of proprietary cell stacking IP that enables density and cost advantages vs competitors using less advanced stacking.

8 · Moat Verdict

Sandisk holds a moderate moat anchored by 30-year brand heritage, Kioxia JV scale, and the secular AI storage demand tailwind.
