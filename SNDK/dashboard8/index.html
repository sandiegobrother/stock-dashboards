Results Commentary
Sandisk's first public quarter as a standalone pure-play NAND company demonstrated the AI enterprise SSD thesis. Enterprise SSD revenue of $1.06B (+55% YoY, +11.6% vs estimate) and 38.2% gross margin (+170bps beat) confirmed that AI data center storage demand is creating a pricing premium for enterprise NAND. The pure-play strategy — focusing entirely on NAND vs the old WD HDD+NAND structure — is showing immediate operational focus benefits.
Our enterprise SSD platform is capturing the AI infrastructure storage opportunity. Hyperscaler demand for high-density, high-performance NAND in AI training clusters has driven our enterprise mix to 56% and continues to grow.
— David Goeckeler, CEO, Q2 FY2026 Earnings Call

Key Metrics Analysis
Enterprise SSD at 56% of revenue (+2pp vs estimate) and 38.2% gross margin (+170bps beat) are the two metrics that define Sandisk's premium positioning. Enterprise SSD commands 3-5x the ASP of commodity NAND — at 56% enterprise mix, Sandisk's blended gross margins are structurally better than pure commodity players. The +28% QoQ bit shipments (vs +22% estimate) confirms supply and demand are both expanding.
