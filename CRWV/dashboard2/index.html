Revenue Analysis
Nebius revenue growth is extraordinary — from $13.5M (2022) to an annualized ~$312M run rate in H1 2025 (+545% YoY in H1). The NVIDIA $2B strategic investment in late 2024 was the transformative catalyst, providing both capital and — critically — priority access to NVIDIA's next-generation Rubin GPU architecture. The $7-9B end-2026 ARR target is the most ambitious forward guidance on this list. Management's $20B+ contracted demand backlog provides the justification — demand clearly exists. The question is execution speed: can Nebius build, deploy, and operate 5GW+ of data center infrastructure at the pace required?

Net Income Analysis
Nebius's GAAP financials are distorted by large non-cash items — the H1 2025 $398M GAAP gain primarily reflects fair value changes on the NVIDIA warrant and investment received. The adjusted net loss of -$175M for H1 2025 (annualized ~-$350M) is the true economic picture: a company burning cash to build hyperscale AI data centers. Adjusted EBITDA loss improved significantly — from -$116.5M (H1 2024) to -$74.7M (H1 2025), a -36% reduction, showing improving unit economics as revenue scales. Losses are entirely expected and appropriate for this stage of infrastructure buildout.

Margin Analysis
Gross margin evolution tells the story of Nebius's maturation: negative in 2022-2023 (fixed costs exceeded early revenues), recovering to +38% in 2024, and reaching ~45% in H1 2025. The trajectory toward 50%+ in 2026 reflects GPU cluster utilization improvement — each incremental customer served on existing infrastructure has near-zero marginal cost. AI cloud gross margins of 50%+ are achievable at scale — CoreWeave achieves ~40% at current scale, AWS achieves 60%+ at maturity.
