Why Institutions Buy

$553B RPO — Most Visible Revenue Pipeline in Enterprise Software
REVENUE CERTAINTY
Oracle's $553B Remaining Performance Obligations (+325% YoY) represents the most contracted forward revenue of any enterprise software company. This backlog converts to 20%+ organic revenue growth for 3+ years regardless of new order intake. Enterprise software investors who model revenue certainty rank Oracle's visibility as uniquely low-risk for a 20%+ growth company.
B2
OCI Positioned as 4th US AI Hyperscaler via Stargate
GROWTH
Oracle's $100B+ Stargate AI partnership with OpenAI, SoftBank, and MGX is positioning OCI as the AI cloud of choice for OpenAI workloads. OCI at 3% cloud market share has nowhere to go but up — and the Stargate partnership provides the anchor customer relationship that AWS and GCP cannot compete for (they're competitors). Institutional technology investors value the competitive asymmetry.
B3
Oracle Database Autonomous — Legacy Customer Migration Revenue
MOAT
Oracle's on-premise database installed base (the most mission-critical enterprise data infrastructure) migrating to Oracle Cloud generates high-margin, recurring subscription revenue. The switching cost moat for Oracle Database users is measured in years and tens of millions of dollars of migration cost — creating highly predictable cloud revenue from a captive $30B+ on-premise installed base.
Why Institutions Avoid
A1
$94B Debt + Negative FY2025 FCF — Leverage Constraint
FINANCIAL RISK
$94B in gross debt with FY2025 FCF of -$9.9B (due to $48B capex) creates the highest leverage profile in enterprise software. Investment-grade credit-constrained funds (insurance companies, pension funds with leverage restrictions) are forced to reduce or avoid Oracle until FCF recovers positive. FCF recovery from negative to $10B+ by FY2027 is the critical de-risking event.
A2
AWS/Azure/GCP Competition — OCI at 3% Market Share vs Incumbents
COMPETITION
AWS (32%), Azure (22%), GCP (12%) have 20+ year head starts in cloud infrastructure and AI services. Oracle OCI winning against these incumbents requires customers to choose the 4th-place provider for new AI workloads — a credible but challenging argument when hyperscalers are competing aggressively on price and services breadth.

Investment Thesis
Long-Term Investment Thesis · ORCL · Portfolio Manager Perspective
Oracle's investment thesis has fundamentally changed in the past 18 months — from a legacy database company managing decline to an AI cloud infrastructure company with unprecedented revenue visibility. The $553B RPO growing 325% YoY is the single most important financial disclosure in enterprise technology — it represents 8+ years of contracted revenue at current conversion rates. The Stargate partnership is strategically differentiated: Oracle is the AI cloud provider that OpenAI chose because AWS and GCP are direct competitors to OpenAI's business model. FCF recovery from -$9.9B (FY2025) to $15B+ (FY2028) is the primary financial re-rating catalyst. The $94B debt is the legitimate near-term concern — but at $38B+ annual FCF by FY2027, deleveraging capacity is substantial.
