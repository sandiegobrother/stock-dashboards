Results Commentary
Tesla's Q4 2025 was the weakest earnings report of the Robotaxi era, with deliveries declining -8.6% YoY for the full year — the first annual delivery decline in company history. Auto gross margin compression to 17.1% (from 29% in 2022) is the most concerning fundamental trend. The EPS miss of $0.73 vs $0.76 expected was modest in absolute terms, but the combination of delivery miss, margin compression, and vague guidance created significant investor concern. Energy storage (+48% YoY) was the lone bright spot.

Key Metrics Analysis
The delivery miss (495,570 vs 510,000 expected) was the headline negative — but the more concerning metric was auto gross margin at 17.1%, down 110bps vs estimates. Price cuts used to defend market share against BYD are directly impressing margins. FCF of $1.6B was well below the $2.0B estimate, raising questions about capital allocation for Cybercab and Optimus investment. The Energy segment ($3.1B, +48%) remains strong and partially offsets auto weakness.

Guidance Analysis
The absence of specific delivery or revenue guidance for 2026 was the most concerning element of the call. 'Growth over 2025' implies at least 1.78M+ deliveries (vs 1.77M in 2025), but the range is so wide as to be nearly meaningless. The Cybercab timeline moving from 'mid-2026' to 'late 2026' added to concerns about execution pace. Auto gross margin guidance was vague — the market wanted a specific path back to 20%+, and management didn't provide one.

The -5.1% after-hours drop reflected investor frustration with the delivery miss and vague guidance — particularly the Cybercab timeline extension to 'late 2026.' Bulls had expected a more specific Robotaxi commercial timeline to offset the fundamental auto weakness. The stock had been elevated on Robotaxi optionality, and the earnings call provided little new evidence that the Robotaxi thesis was on a clear commercial trajectory.
