Revenue Analysis
Tesla revenue grew from $53.8B (2021) to $97.7B (2025) — near-doubling — but stalled in the final two years as BYD's rise and price wars compressed the relationship between volume and revenue. The energy storage segment is the emerging second engine: +67% YoY in Q3 2025, with Megapack becoming a standalone multi-billion dollar business. The automotive margin story is under pressure, but the energy + services + AI optionality makes Tesla's financial future non-linear.

Net Income Analysis
Tesla's net income peaked at $15B in 2023 and declined to ~$5.4B in 2025 — a 64% drop in two years. The margin compression is structural until either demand recovers, new affordable models launch, or Robotaxi/FSD monetization begins. The market is not pricing Tesla on current earnings — it is pricing Robotaxi, Optimus, and AI/energy optionality.

Margin Analysis
Tesla's margins have been on a declining trend since 2021 peaks — driven by aggressive price cuts to defend market share against BYD. The 17% gross margin in 2025 is near the lowest in Tesla's public history. Recovery requires successful Robotaxi monetization, an affordable new model, or demand recovery. Energy segment margins (54%+) provide a structural counterweight as that business scales.

Is Tesla, Inc. Financially Strong or Weakening? — Definitive Assessment
Tesla's financials tell a story of peak-and-compression from 2022-2023 highs. Revenue stalled, net income fell 64% from peak, and FCF halved. The $36B net cash balance sheet is the key financial anchor. The market values Tesla on Robotaxi, Optimus, and energy optionality — not current earnings. If those materialize, the financial picture transforms completely. If they don't, current financials cannot justify the valuation. Financial health: B. Trend: Auto weakening / Energy strengthening.
