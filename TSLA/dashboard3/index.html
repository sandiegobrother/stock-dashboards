3 · Switching Costs
Switching Costs · Score 5/10
5

The Supercharger network makes owning a non-NACS vehicle a step backward in charging convenience — a physical switching cost that grows as the network expands.

4 · Cost Advantage
Cost Advantage · Score 6/10
6
Tesla's auto cost advantage has eroded against BYD. BYD's vertical integration (batteries, motors, electronics all in-house) produces a Seagull at $10,000 vs Tesla's cheapest at $35,000+ — this is a different market segment, but BYD is moving upmarket rapidly. Tesla retains cost advantage vs Western OEMs (GM, Ford, VW) but the competitive frame has shifted.

5 · Patents & Proprietary Technology
IP & Proprietary Tech · Score 8/10
8

Dojo — Tesla's purpose-built AI training supercomputer — processes this unique real-world dataset, creating a compound moat where more vehicles → more data → better FSD → more sales → more vehicles. Optimus humanoid represents $1T+ TAM optionality if commercialized.

8 · Moat Verdict
Competitive Advantage Verdict · TSLA · Overall Moat Score: 7/10 · Narrow-Wide Moat
Tesla holds a powerful but narrowing moat in core EV manufacturing, offset by expanding strength in FSD data, energy storage, and Supercharger infrastructure. Brand remains exceptional but uniquely CEO-dependent. BYD's structural cost advantage in low-to-mid range vehicles is a genuine long-term challenge.
