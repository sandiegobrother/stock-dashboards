Robotaxi — Cybercab Commercial Launch
New Products
VERY HIGH
Tesla's Cybercab (purpose-built robotaxi) is targeting commercial launch in Austin TX in 2026 at $0.20-0.30/mile cost — compared to Uber/Lyft's $1.50-2.00/mile. A successful Robotaxi network generating $10B+ revenue by 2027 would completely transform Tesla's revenue mix from hardware-dependent to high-margin software/services. This is the single most important growth catalyst.

Industry Growth Context
Tesla's TAM is almost incomprehensibly large if Robotaxi and Optimus succeed — transportation is a $10T+ annual market globally. The key insight is that Tesla is transitioning from a hardware company (cars) to a software/services company (FSD, Robotaxi) with hardware as distribution. The robotaxi market alone is projected at $600B-1T by 2035. Energy storage is a guaranteed growth driver regardless of Robotaxi outcome.

Growth Verdict
Growth Potential Verdict · TSLA · +15% → +40% Est. Revenue Growth 2-4 Years
Tesla's growth potential is the most binary of any large-cap stock. The bull case ($200B+ revenue by FY2028) requires Robotaxi to scale commercially and Optimus to ship 100K+ units — transforming Tesla from a $97B auto company into a $200B+ tech/services company. The base case ($125B) assumes modest Robotaxi progress and continued Energy storage growth. The bear case ($105B) requires Robotaxi delays and continued EV market share pressure. Energy storage growing 65%+ annually is the reliable growth driver regardless of Robotaxi outcome.
