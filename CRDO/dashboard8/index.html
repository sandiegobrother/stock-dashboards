Results Commentary
Credo's Q3 FY2026 demonstrated the AEC de facto standard thesis in financial terms. Revenue of $185M (+193% YoY) beat the $172M estimate by 7.6%, while the 68.6% non-GAAP gross margin (+360bps vs estimate) was the most important positive surprise. The record gross margin proves Credo's AEC products command genuine pricing power — a pure-play monopoly in a technology category where hyperscalers have no alternatives.

Key Metrics Analysis
The 68.6% non-GAAP gross margin is the single most important metric — it is the highest in Credo's history and confirms that AEC pricing power is genuine, not just volume-driven. Non-GAAP gross margins above 65% are typically associated with software companies, not semiconductor hardware — Credo is generating software-like economics from silicon. The $1.31B cash balance (zero debt) provides runway for R&D investment without dilution.
