Why Institutions Buy
B1
De Facto AEC Standard — No Production Competition Through 2027
MONOPOLY
Credo is the only company shipping Active Electrical Cable (AEC) products at hyperscale volume — making it the de facto standard for AI data center intra-rack connectivity. Design wins at multiple hyperscalers with 3-5 year design cycle visibility create contracted revenue that competitors cannot quickly displace. The 'de facto standard' positioning in a high-growth semiconductor segment is the most valuable competitive position available.
B2
Three Consecutive Revenue Near-Triplings — Track Record Established
EXECUTION
FY2024: +70%, FY2025: +126%, FY2026E: +220% — three consecutive years of revenue near-tripling establishes an execution track record that institutional momentum investors find compelling. Very few semiconductor companies have tripled revenue for three consecutive years. The track record reduces the risk discount that early-stage hypergrowth typically requires.
B3
40% Below Analyst Consensus — Largest Discount on This List
VALUATION
Credo at $35 vs analyst average target of $60 represents a 40% discount to consensus — one of the largest mispricing gaps among AI semiconductor companies. When 20+ analysts who cover AI connectivity semiconductors collectively set targets 40% above current prices, the fundamental case for undervaluation is difficult to ignore.

Long-Term Investment Thesis · CRDO · Portfolio Manager Perspective
Credo's investment thesis rests on three pillars: monopoly technology position, track record execution, and significant valuation discount. The AEC de facto standard with no production competitor is the rarest competitive position in semiconductors. Three consecutive revenue near-triplings prove management can execute at hypergrowth scale. The insider selling is the legitimate concern — but CEO/Board equity positions remain substantial, and the selling pattern is consistent with normal lockup-driven diversification.
