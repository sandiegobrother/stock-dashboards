MOAT
Broadcom's VMware acquisition created the most durable enterprise software moat in infrastructure technology. 300,000+ enterprises cannot migrate away from VMware virtualization in 1-2 years regardless of pricing — it's mission-critical infrastructure embedded in every data center. The ~98% renewal rate proves customers are paying the new subscription pricing despite vocal objections. 10-15 year lock-in at premium economics.

This is contracted, multi-year revenue with 3-5 year design cycle visibility — the most predictable large-scale revenue in semiconductors.
B3
Hock Tan Capital Allocation — 10-for-10 Track Record
MANAGEMENT
CEO Hock Tan has a 100% track record of value-accretive acquisitions (Broadcom, CA Technologies, Symantec, VMware) — each generating FCF returns well above the acquisition cost of capital. For institutional investors who judge management quality by capital allocation, Broadcom is in a rare category of perfectly executed M&A compounders.
B4
PEG 0.64 on Non-GAAP Earnings — Structurally Cheap
VALUATION
Non-GAAP forward P/E of 26x with FY2026E earnings growing 30%+ gives a PEG of 0.64 — below 1.0, signaling statistical cheapness. The GAAP P/E of 67x misleads due to non-cash acquisition amortization. Sophisticated institutional investors who model non-GAAP economics find Broadcom is among the cheapest compounders in large-cap technology.
Why Institutions Avoid
A1
$94B Debt — Highest Leverage in Semiconductor Sector
FINANCIAL RISK
At 3.1x net debt/EBITDA, Broadcom carries the highest absolute debt load in semiconductors. Fixed income-sensitive funds, risk-parity strategies, and conservative institutional mandates may exclude Broadcom due to leverage constraints. In a rising-rate environment, $4B+ in annual interest expense becomes a material earnings headwind.
A2
VMware Customer Backlash — Churn Risk Not Zero
EXECUTION RISK
Major enterprises including AT&T, Sky, and others have publicly explored VMware alternatives (OpenShift, Nutanix) in response to Broadcom's aggressive pricing. While ~98% renewal rates hold currently, migrations take 18-36 months — the wave of potential churn from 2023-2024 repricing decisions may not hit revenue until 2025-2026. Funds with 3-5 year horizons worry about 2027-2028 churn acceleration.
A3
GAAP P/E 67x — Index Inclusion and Mandate Constraints
TECHNICAL
Many institutional mandates exclude companies with GAAP losses or very high GAAP P/E ratios from value or quality-screened portfolios. The 67x GAAP P/E from acquisition amortization means Broadcom fails many quantitative screens, artificially limiting the institutional buyer universe.

Broadcom is a high-conviction 3-5 year compounder that should be a core infrastructure technology holding.
