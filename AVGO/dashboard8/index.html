Results Commentary
Broadcom's Q1 FY2026 results demonstrated the power of its dual-engine model: AI semiconductor revenue growing +220% YoY alongside VMware software compounding at +22% YoY. The AI revenue of $4.1B in a single quarter — annualizing to $16B+ — is on pace to exceed even the upgraded FY2026 AI guidance of $90B+ when combined with the AI networking revenue. VMware's ~98% renewal rates confirm that enterprise switching costs are intact despite customer backlash about pricing increases. Non-GAAP gross margins of 79.1% are among the highest in semiconductors.
Our AI revenue surged 220% year-on-year, driven by the rapid expansion of hyperscale XPU deployments. The infrastructure software segment continues to demonstrate the resilience of our subscription model.
— Hock Tan, CEO, Q1 FY2026 Earnings Call

Key Metrics Analysis
The AI revenue beat (+7.9% vs estimates) is the most important metric — it confirms that Google TPU and Meta MTIA custom ASIC volumes are ramping faster than modeled. The non-GAAP gross margin of 79.1% is exceptional for a capital-intensive semiconductor business. VMware ARR beat confirms conversion progress. The net debt reduction trajectory ($63B vs $65B estimate) indicates Broadcom is deleveraging faster than expected — a positive signal for future dividend increases and share buybacks.
