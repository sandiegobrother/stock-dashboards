#1
FY2025 EBITDA Guide Miss — Credibility Deficit
Financial
CRITICAL
9/10
55%
Tempus guided 'slightly positive' adj. EBITDA for FY2025 and delivered -$7.4M — a miss that damaged management credibility with institutional investors. The new FY2026 $65M adj. EBITDA guide must be delivered to restore confidence. Another miss would likely trigger multiple compression below 18x P/Revenue toward sector comparable multiples of 10-12x.
Mitigant: Q4 2025 adj. EBITDA of $12.9M (+760% vs Q3) demonstrates the trajectory is real. If Q4 momentum sustains across all four quarters of FY2026, the $65M guide is achievable.
#2
HIPAA and Patient Data Privacy Regulatory Risk
Regulatory
HIGH
8/10
35%
Tempus's 7M+ patient records — the core asset — are subject to HIPAA, GDPR, and state-level health data privacy laws. A major data breach or regulatory finding that Tempus used protected health information without adequate consent could trigger massive fines, class action lawsuits, and loss of hospital data partnerships that are irreplaceable.
Mitigant: Tempus invests heavily in de-identification, consent management, and security infrastructure. HIPAA compliance is central to the business model — hospital partnerships require it.
#3
Pharma Data Licensing Revenue Durability
Industry Disruption
HIGH
7/10
40%
Tempus's AI data licensing revenue (Illumina, AstraZeneca, and 40+ pharma partners) is the highest-margin revenue stream — but AI-generated synthetic data could eventually reduce pharmaceutical companies' dependency on real-world patient data. If AI models can generate sufficiently realistic synthetic clinical trial data, the $200M+ data licensing market contracts.
Mitigant: Regulatory agencies (FDA) require real-world clinical data for drug approval — synthetic data cannot fully substitute for 5-10 years. Tempus's multimodal data (genomics, imaging, clinical notes combined) is uniquely valuable.
#4
Hospital Partnership Concentration Risk
Economic
HIGH
7/10
30%
Tempus's data partnerships with major academic medical centers (University of Chicago, Mayo Clinic affiliates) are the source of the 7M patient records. Loss of a major hospital partnership due to contract expiration, competitive bidding, or policy changes would impair both data growth and the revenue sharing arrangements.
Mitigant: Multi-year exclusive data partnership agreements create lock-in. Tempus's clinical trial matching and MRD testing provide revenue back to hospital partners, aligning interests.
#5
Reimbursement Risk for Molecular Residual Disease Testing
Regulatory
HIGH
7/10
45%
MRD testing revenue (+56% QoQ) is the fastest-growing segment but depends on insurance reimbursement expanding to more cancer types and patient populations. CMS and private payers are evaluating MRD reimbursement on a case-by-case basis — an adverse national coverage determination could impair the growth trajectory.
Mitigant: Clinical evidence supporting MRD testing's utility in treatment decisions is growing rapidly. FDA approval pathways for companion diagnostic uses are being developed.
#6
Competition from Foundation Medicine (Roche) and Guardant
Competition
MEDIUM
6/10
50%
Foundation Medicine (owned by Roche) and Guardant Health both compete in genomic testing and pharma data services. Roche's financial resources and global distribution could enable aggressive pricing to win pharma data contracts.
Mitigant: Tempus's multimodal approach (combining genomics with imaging, pathology, and clinical notes) is differentiated from pure genomics competitors like Foundation Medicine.
