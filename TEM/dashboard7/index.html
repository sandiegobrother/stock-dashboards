Why Institutions Buy
B1
7M Patient Records — Irreplaceable Multimodal Data Moat
UNIQUE ASSET
Tempus's 7M+ patient records combining genomics, imaging, pathology, and clinical notes is the largest multimodal precision medicine dataset in existence — and it took 15 years to build. No competitor, regardless of funding, can replicate 15 years of hospital partnership data accumulation in 3-5 years. Healthcare AI specialists rank this as the most defensible data moat in medical technology.
B2
MRD Testing +56% QoQ — Growth Engine Accelerating
GROWTH
Molecular Residual Disease testing volume grew 56% in a single quarter — the fastest growing segment in a company already growing 83% annually. MRD's clinical utility (detecting cancer recurrence before imaging) is being validated by reimbursement expansion and physician adoption. Institutional oncology-focused healthcare funds are accumulating Tempus specifically on MRD trajectory.
B3
40+ Pharma Partners — Data Licensing Machine
RECURRING REVENUE
40+ pharmaceutical companies paying recurring data licensing fees create near-100% margin recurring revenue that is uncorrelated to device or procedure volume. Institutional healthcare investors model the pharma licensing as a compounding recurring revenue stream that will eventually anchor Tempus's valuation similarly to data analytics companies.
Why Institutions Avoid
A1
EBITDA Guide Miss — FY2025 Missed 'Slightly Positive' Target
CREDIBILITY
Missing the FY2025 adj. EBITDA guidance (-$7.4M vs 'slightly positive') has created institutional credibility concerns about management's ability to forecast profitability. Investors who allocate based on management guidance are re-rating Tempus from 'execution credible' to 'show me' — requiring actual FY2026 EBITDA delivery before committing capital.
A2
HIPAA and Data Privacy Existential Risk
TAIL RISK
A major HIPAA violation or data breach could sever hospital partnerships that are the foundation of the 7M record moat. Healthcare data privacy regulations are among the most stringent and liability-laden in any industry. Healthcare compliance-focused institutions maintain position limits specifically for data-intensive health companies.
