Why Institutions Buy
B1
Novo Nordisk Exclusive — GLP-1 DTC Distribution Partnership
TRANSFORMATIONAL DEAL
Hims's exclusive DTC distribution agreement with Novo Nordisk positions it as the preferred online channel for the world's leading GLP-1 pharmaceutical company. Novo chose Hims specifically because of its 2.2M subscribers with proven willingness to pay online for health services. This partnership provides regulatory legitimacy (no more compounding pharmacy risk) and access to the most explosive pharmaceutical category of the decade.
B2
~80% Gross Margin — Software Economics on Healthcare
QUALITY
Hims's ~80% gross margins on a healthcare platform reflect that the marginal cost of an additional telehealth prescription approaches zero. Once the platform is built and licensed, each new subscriber at $80/month is near-pure margin. Institutional software investors find this healthcare delivery model compelling: it looks like SaaS economics applied to medicine.
B3
2.2M Subscribers — Cross-Sell Engine Pre-Built
PLATFORM
2.2M subscribers who already trust Hims with sensitive health conditions (hair loss, ED, mental health) represent a pre-built distribution network for GLP-1 products. The subscriber acquisition cost has been amortized — new product categories generate nearly incremental revenue. Institutional growth investors model cross-sell economics that make the GLP-1 revenue ramp exceptionally capital-efficient.
Why Institutions Avoid
A1
8.2% Short Interest — Highest on This List
HIGH SHORT
Hims carries 8.2% short interest — the highest of any company on this watchlist — reflecting the FDA compounding crackdown risk. Bears have a clear thesis: FDA eliminates compounding GLP-1 revenue, Novo deal fails to scale, revenue growth stalls. The high short interest creates volatility risk in both directions — any positive catalyst creates a short squeeze; any negative development accelerates the sell-off.
A2
FDA Compounding Enforcement — Revenue Cliff Risk
REGULATORY
The FDA's declared end of the semaglutide shortage and enforcement action against compounding pharmacies already caused a 25%+ stock decline in early 2025. Remaining compounded GLP-1 revenue is at ongoing risk of FDA enforcement. Regulatory-sensitive healthcare institutions maintain reduced positions until the Novo deal revenue replaces compounding revenue completely.

Investment Thesis
Long-Term Investment Thesis · HIMS · Portfolio Manager Perspective
Hims is a high-risk, high-reward institutional bet on the Novo Nordisk partnership and GLP-1 DTC distribution. The ~80% gross margins, 2.2M subscribers, zero debt, and $929M cash create a fundamentally strong platform. The Novo exclusive deal is the transformational catalyst that removes the regulatory risk (no more compounding dependency) while adding access to the most important pharmaceutical category of the decade. The 8.2% short interest creates an asymmetric setup: if Novo GLP-1 revenue scales to $500M+ annually, shorts cover explosively and the stock could double. The FDA compounding risk is the bear case — but the Novo deal is specifically designed to address this risk.
