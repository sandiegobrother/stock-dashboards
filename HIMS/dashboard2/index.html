Revenue Analysis
Hims & Hers has delivered one of the most remarkable revenue growth trajectories in consumer healthcare — from $271M (2021) to $2.35B (2025), a 9x increase in four years. Each year saw 59-94% growth, making the compounding effect exceptional. The GLP-1 weight loss subscription became the accelerant in 2023-2024, layered on top of the highly profitable sexual health, dermatology, and mental health core verticals. Management confirmed in the Q4 2025 call that the majority of revenue and profitability comes from non-weight-loss products — Hims is a diversified platform, not a single-product bet. The landmark Novo Nordisk distribution deal announced March 9, 2026 removes the biggest regulatory overhang and creates a formal branded GLP-1 distribution partnership.

Net Income Analysis
Hims moved from GAAP losses of -$183M (2022) to +$128M GAAP net income (2025) — a $311M improvement in three years. The second consecutive year of GAAP profitability ($126M in 2024, $128M in 2025) confirms the model has matured past the investment phase. Note: Q3 2025 net income was only $15.8M vs Q3 2024's $75.6M — but the prior year included a $60.8M tax benefit. Adjusting for that, Q3 2025 underlying profitability was actually stronger. Non-GAAP net income of $318M in FY2025 reflects the true earnings power of the subscription platform.

Free Cash Flow Analysis
Hims generated its first positive FCF in 2023 ($55M) and scaled to $185M in 2024 — a 236% jump in one year. FY2025 FCF was impacted by Q2's negative FCF (-$69M) related to inventory and working capital investments tied to GLP-1 product transitions, but Q1 and Q3 were each strongly positive at $79M each. The $929M cash balance and zero debt mean the company has extraordinary financial flexibility — the Eucalyptus acquisition ($1.15B) will be funded from cash reserves without adding debt.

Margin Analysis
Gross margins tell the story of Hims's two-phase evolution. Phase 1 (2022-2024): margins expanded from 77% to 82% as fixed platform costs were leveraged over growing subscription revenue. Phase 2 (2025): gross margin compressed to 74% due to GLP-1 product mix changes (lower-margin compounded injectables, then FDA-mandated product transitions). Q3 2025's 74% gross margin vs 79% in Q3 2024 is this compression in action. The core sexual health and dermatology verticals maintain 80%+ gross margins — the compression is temporary and product-mix driven, not structural.

Debt Analysis
Hims has never borrowed a dollar in its public life — entirely self-funded through FCF generation and equity capital. The $929M cash position is extraordinary for a company of this scale and stage. The zero-debt model provides complete financial freedom to invest, acquire, and weather regulatory uncertainty.

Is Hims & Hers Health, Inc. Financially Strong or Weakening? — Definitive Assessment
Hims & Hers is financially one of the most impressive DTC healthcare transformations of the past decade. Revenue grew 9x, gross margins reached 82%, and GAAP profitability was achieved and sustained. The zero-debt balance sheet with $929M cash is exceptional for a growth-stage company. The Novo Nordisk partnership fundamentally changes the regulatory landscape — converting from legal combatant to branded distribution partner. The key near-term risks are the SEC investigation and gross margin compression as GLP-1 product mix evolves. But the core economics — subscription model, 80%+ gross margins, positive FCF — are durable. Financial health: B+. Trend: Strengthening. Novo deal is the potential re-rating catalyst of 2026.
