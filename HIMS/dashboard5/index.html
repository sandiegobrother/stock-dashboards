#1
FDA Compounding Pharmacy GLP-1 Crackdown
Regulatory
CRITICAL
10/10
65%
Hims & Hers was generating significant GLP-1 revenue through compounding pharmacies during the FDA-declared semaglutide shortage. The FDA declared the shortage ended in early 2025, triggering enforcement action against compounding pharmacies — the stock fell 25%+ on this news. Future FDA enforcement could eliminate remaining compounded GLP-1 revenue entirely.
Mitigant: The Novo Nordisk exclusive partnership provides a legitimate, branded GLP-1 distribution channel. FDA-approved products are not subject to compounding restrictions.
#2
Novo Nordisk Partnership Execution
Industry Disruption
HIGH
8/10
40%
The Novo Nordisk exclusive DTC distribution deal is the transformational catalyst — but it has not yet generated material revenue. If Novo Nordisk renegotiates terms, limits volume allocations, or the partnership fails to scale, Hims loses the primary GLP-1 revenue replacement thesis. The deal structure (exclusivity terms, pricing, volume commitments) is not fully public.
Mitigant: Novo Nordisk signed the deal specifically because Hims has 2.2M subscribers with proven willingness to pay for health services online. Novo needs DTC distribution expertise that Hims has.
#3
Regulatory Binary — State-Level Telehealth Laws
Regulatory
HIGH
8/10
50%
Several states are passing or considering restrictions on telehealth prescribing — particularly for controlled substances and weight loss medications prescribed without in-person visits. A patchwork of state-level restrictions could impair Hims's ability to prescribe in key markets, fragmenting the national DTC model.
Mitigant: Hims has invested in hybrid care models (in-person referrals, lab testing) that strengthen the clinical credibility of the telehealth prescription pathway.
#4
GLP-1 Pricing Compression
Economic
HIGH
7/10
55%
As GLP-1 supply normalizes and Novo Nordisk/Eli Lilly ramp manufacturing, GLP-1 street prices are declining. If Ozempic/Wegovy become widely available at pharmacy chains at reduced out-of-pocket costs, the DTC premium model that Hims charges may be harder to sustain.
Mitigant: Hims's subscription model (ongoing telehealth consultations, medication management) provides value beyond just drug access that pharmacy chains cannot replicate.
#5
Competition from Ro Health, Noom, Calibrate
Competition
MEDIUM
6/10
50%
Multiple well-funded DTC healthcare competitors (Ro, Noom Medical, Calibrate) are competing for the same GLP-1-eligible patient population. Subscriber acquisition costs are increasing as the category becomes more competitive.
Mitigant: Hims's 2.2M existing subscribers and brand recognition in sensitive health categories (hair loss, ED, mental health) create cross-sell advantages that pure GLP-1 competitors lack.
#6
Testosterone and ED Market Commoditization
Industry Disruption
MEDIUM
5/10
55%
Hims's original core products (sildenafil/ED, minoxidil/hair loss) are rapidly commoditizing as generic competitors and retail pharmacy DTC models proliferate. These legacy revenue streams face ongoing margin pressure.
Mitigant: GLP-1 expansion creates a new high-value category that replaces legacy revenue. Legacy products drive subscriber acquisition that cross-sells higher-margin offerings.

Risk Verdict
Risk Summary · HIMS · ELEVATED RISK · 1 Critical · 3 High Risk Factors
Hims has the highest regulatory binary risk of any ticker on this list. The FDA compounding crackdown is not a hypothetical — it already happened, causing a 25%+ stock decline. The entire bull thesis now rests on the Novo Nordisk partnership converting to scale revenue. Most dangerous risk: FDA expanding compounding enforcement to all GLP-1 adjacent products + Novo Nordisk partnership delayed or limited in scope simultaneously.
