1 · Brand Strength
Brand Assessment · Score 9/10
9
Lululemon pioneered the premium athleisure category and commands pricing power that Nike and Adidas cannot match in yoga/running/training premium. The 'lululemon educator' model — trained brand ambassadors who are athletes and community leaders — creates grassroots brand authenticity that advertising spend cannot replicate. However, Alo Yoga and Vuori have emerged as credible challengers to lululemon's premium positioning.

2 · Network Effects
Network Effects · Score 6/10
6
Lululemon's community-based marketing creates network effects through sweat collectives, educator programs, and in-store events that make the brand a lifestyle identity, not just a product. 18M+ loyalty program members sharing activity data and engaging with lululemon content create a flywheel where engaged members spend 3-5x more than non-members.

3 · Switching Costs
Switching Costs · Score 5/10
5
Switching costs are primarily identity-based — lululemon customers who have adopted the brand as part of their athlete identity face a 'brand self-concept' switching cost. The 57% gross margin proves these identity-based switching costs translate into real pricing power — customers consistently pay 2-3x the functional cost of equivalent athletic apparel.

5 · Patents & Proprietary Technology
IP & Proprietary Tech · Score 7/10
7
Lululemon's proprietary fabric technologies (Luon, Nulu, Everlux) are developed through the 'Science of Feel' R&D program and provide differentiated performance characteristics that competitors struggle to exactly replicate. The buttery-soft feel of Nulu fabric and the 4-way stretch of Luon are brand-defining experiences that command premium pricing — each SKU is a proprietary formulation.

8 · Moat Verdict
Competitive Advantage Verdict · LULU · Overall Moat Score: 7/10 · Narrow-Wide Moat
Lululemon holds a genuine but narrower moat than its peak years suggest — premium athleisure brand equity, proprietary fabrics, and 57% gross margins are durable advantages. The US market is maturing against Alo/Vuori competition, while China represents a multi-decade expansion opportunity. The new CEO is the key moat-widening or moat-narrowing catalyst. Moat Score: 7/10. Narrow Moat. Brand premium + fabric IP are durable; US competitive intensity is the moat risk. New CEO product direction is the decisive variable.
