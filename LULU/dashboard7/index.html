Why Institutions Buy
B1
22x P/E — 10-Year Low on Best-in-Class Premium Retail
DEEP VALUE
lululemon at 22x forward P/E is at its lowest valuation in a decade, and at parity with Nike (21x) despite having 13% higher gross margins and superior brand positioning. Contrarian institutional value investors are buying the dislocation between brand quality (unchanged) and valuation (multiyear low). The metric that matters most — China growing 46% — is telling institutions that the global brand thesis is intact.
B2
China +46% Growth — Brand Globally Intact
GROWTH
While US comps have decelerated, China growing 46% YoY proves lululemon's brand resonance is a global, not just domestic, phenomenon. China at only ~100 stores with 500+ targeted represents 5-7 years of store expansion ahead in a market with growing premium consumer spend. Institutional Asia specialists view China as a separate, fully-funded growth story within lululemon.
B3
New CEO = Product Innovation Reset Catalyst
CATALYST
The new CEO appointment — which prompted a 9% positive market reaction — removes the strategic uncertainty that was the primary reason for the 22x P/E discount. A product-focused CEO with premium retail experience has a clear mandate: re-accelerate the US product innovation cycle that made lululemon dominant in the first place. Institutions bought the announcement because they believe product cycles are mean-reverting.
B4
57% Gross Margin + Zero Debt = Financial Fortress
BALANCE SHEET
lululemon's combination of industry-best 57% gross margins and a net cash position ($1.5B) creates the most resilient financial profile in premium retail. Even if US comps decline modestly in 2026, the financial fortress ensures lululemon can invest in China expansion, product development, and buybacks simultaneously without capital markets dependency.
Why Institutions Avoid
A1
5.8% Short Interest — Highest vs Premium Retail Peers
SENTIMENT
lululemon carries 5.8% short interest — well above premium retail peers — reflecting genuine institutional concern about US competitive dynamics. Alo Yoga and Vuori's aggressive expansion, combined with Nike's Under Armour-like identity crisis narrative, has created a short thesis that institutional bears are actively running. Higher short interest implies the market is more skeptical than consensus implies.
A2
US Same-Store Sales Deceleration — Growth Story Transition
EXECUTION RISK
US SSS near 0% growth is a significant deceleration from the 10-15% historical average. For growth-mandate institutional funds that require consistent domestic comparable sales growth, lululemon fails the screen until the new CEO delivers a product cycle improvement. The growth-to-value transition creates a period of ownership gap before the re-rating occurs.

Investment Thesis
Long-Term Investment Thesis · LULU · Portfolio Manager Perspective
lululemon at 22x P/E (10-year low) for a brand with 57% gross margins, zero debt, and China growing 46% represents a compelling value opportunity in premium retail. The market is pricing the US competitive pressure (Alo Yoga, Vuori) as a permanent structural impairment of a brand that has never once lost its premium positioning globally. China's 46% growth rate is the strongest counter-evidence to the 'brand is declining' thesis. The new CEO appointment has removed the strategic uncertainty. Now the question is: does the new CEO deliver product innovation? Historical precedent (Nike under Parker, Under Armour recovery attempts) suggests brand moats in premium apparel are more durable than the market gives credit for. Buy the quality, not the narrative: 22x P/E for 57% gross margins and China growing 46% is a clear asymmetric entry point.
