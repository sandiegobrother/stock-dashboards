#1
US Market Share Erosion — Alo Yoga and Vuori
Competition
HIGH
8/10
55%
Alo Yoga and Vuori have both raised $500M+ at multi-billion dollar valuations, specifically targeting lululemon's core customer with premium athletic apparel. lululemon's US comparable store sales growth was essentially flat in H2 2025 — a stark contrast to its historical 10-15% comps. If these DTC competitors continue gaining share, lululemon's US revenue could face actual declines.
Mitigant: lululemon has 30+ years of brand equity and community-building infrastructure (ambassador programs, in-store events) that cannot be quickly replicated. New CEO product direction could re-accelerate innovation.
#2
CEO Transition Execution Risk
Industry Disruption
HIGH
7/10
40%
CEO Calvin McDonald's abrupt departure and the new CEO appointment creates a 12-24 month window of strategic uncertainty. The new CEO needs to demonstrate product innovation credibility with the core lululemon customer while simultaneously accelerating China and international expansion. An unsuccessful product cycle launch under new leadership would severely impair the re-rating thesis.
Mitigant: The Board's selection process was deliberate — new CEO has relevant premium retail and international expansion experience. Market responded positively (+9%) to the appointment.
#3
Consumer Spending Slowdown
Economic
HIGH
7/10
45%
lululemon's $120+ leggings and $200+ jackets are discretionary luxury purchases vulnerable to consumer spending compression. The average lululemon customer (35-55 year old professional female) has more spending resilience than mass market, but in a severe recession or housing market correction, luxury athleisure would face meaningful headwinds.
Mitigant: Zero debt and $1.5B net cash means lululemon can weather a demand slowdown without financial distress. The brand's pricing power has historically held through economic cycles.
#4
China Execution Risk
Economic
MEDIUM
6/10
35%
China growing +46% is the primary bull thesis offset to US deceleration — but China's economic recovery has been uneven and consumer confidence volatile. Geopolitical tensions (tariffs, boycotts of Western brands) could impair China momentum at the exact moment lululemon needs it to compensate for US slowdown.
Mitigant: lululemon's Chinese consumer association is with athletic wellness — a category less politically charged than fashion or tech brands. Brand positioning is global and aspirational.
#5
Product Innovation Stagnation
Industry Disruption
MEDIUM
6/10
40%
lululemon's core Align legging product was designed in 2014 — competitors have matched or exceeded the technical fabric performance. Without a material new product innovation (comparable to the original Align legging breakthrough), the brand's functional differentiation diminishes to pure brand identity.
Mitigant: New CEO appointment was specifically motivated by the need for product innovation leadership. lululemon's R&D investment in new fabric technology (Nulu, Everlux) continues.
#6
Supply Chain and Tariff Risk
Regulatory
MEDIUM
5/10
50%
Most lululemon products are manufactured in Vietnam and Cambodia — subject to tariff risk under any US trade policy escalation. A 10-25% tariff increase on apparel imports would either compress gross margins or require price increases that could impair volume.
Mitigant: lululemon is diversifying manufacturing across Bangladesh, Sri Lanka, and other lower-risk geographies. Premium brand pricing power provides some tariff absorption capacity.

Risk Verdict
Risk Summary · LULU · MODERATE RISK · 0 Critical · 3 High Risk Factors
lululemon's risk profile is moderate — the underlying brand is strong but the US competitive pressure and CEO transition create a 12-18 month window of elevated uncertainty. At 22x P/E (10-year low), much of the risk is already priced in. China +46% growth and zero debt provide meaningful cushion. Most dangerous risk: New CEO product cycle failing to resonate, US comp sales turning negative, and China macro deterioration occurring simultaneously.
