#1
IRA Medicare Drug Price Negotiation
Regulatory
CRITICAL
9/10
65%
The Inflation Reduction Act empowers CMS to negotiate prices directly for drugs in Medicare Part D. Tirzepatide (Mounjaro/Zepbound) is eligible for 2026 or 2027 negotiation given its launch date and sales volume. Price reductions of 25-60% for Medicare patients are possible — CMS has negotiated 60-79% discounts in initial rounds for other drugs. With GLP-1 driving $20B+ annually, any negotiated price cut would materially impair revenue.
Mitigant: Lilly is challenging IRA pricing provisions legally. Medicare GLP-1 coverage is still limited — negotiation applies only to Medicare Part D, not commercial insurance.
#2
Novo Nordisk Competition (Semaglutide)
Competition
HIGH
8/10
70%
Novo Nordisk's Ozempic/Wegovy (semaglutide) directly competes with Mounjaro/Zepbound (tirzepatide) for the same GLP-1 obesity and diabetes market. Novo has superior manufacturing scale and a longer commercial track record. SURMOUNT-5 trial data showed Zepbound superior to Wegovy for weight loss, but Novo Nordisk is not standing still — semaglutide pills (Rybelsus) and next-generation compounds are in development.
Mitigant: SURMOUNT-5 superiority data gives Lilly clinical differentiation. Tirzepatide's dual GIP/GLP-1 mechanism appears to be more efficacious than single GLP-1 agonism.
#3
GLP-1 Manufacturing Capacity Constraints
Industry Disruption
HIGH
7/10
45%
Lilly is investing $23B+ in new manufacturing capacity, but GLP-1 supply shortages have already impacted revenue and prescription fulfillment. If manufacturing ramp is delayed or yields are below target, revenue guidance could be missed. The complexity of injectable biologic manufacturing means capacity additions take 2-3 years from investment to production.
Mitigant: Lilly has 7 new manufacturing sites under construction globally. US CHIPS Act-equivalent incentives for domestic pharma manufacturing are supporting the build-out.
#4
GLP-1 Side Effects / Safety Signal
Industry Disruption
HIGH
7/10
20%
A serious undiscovered long-term safety signal in GLP-1 agonists — muscle mass loss, thyroid cancer risk, or other chronic effects — could trigger FDA warning letters or market withdrawal. With 30M+ patients on GLP-1s globally, even a 0.1% serious adverse event rate represents 30,000+ patients and regulatory attention.
Mitigant: GLP-1 class has been in use since 2005 (Byetta) with extensive real-world safety data. Cardiovascular benefit data (SURMOUNT-MMO) adds to the positive risk-benefit profile.
#5
Donanemab (Alzheimer's) Commercial Execution
Industry Disruption
MEDIUM
6/10
45%
Donanemab (Kisunla) received FDA approval in July 2024 for early Alzheimer's, but commercial uptake has been slower than expected due to the complex ARIA monitoring requirement (MRI scans every 6 months) and limited infusion center capacity. If Donanemab fails to reach $5B+ peak revenue, Lilly's diversification thesis is impaired.
Mitigant: Alzheimer's treatment is a de novo market — 6.9M US patients with no existing treatment. Even modest penetration represents enormous revenue.
#6
Biosimilar GLP-1 Entry
Competition
MEDIUM
5/10
30%
Tirzepatide's US patent protection runs to approximately 2036, but compounding pharmacies have been formulating 'semaglutide' copies during shortage periods. FDA's crackdown on compounded GLP-1s is ongoing but enforcement is imperfect. Longer-term, biosimilar entry in the late 2030s would compress margins.
Mitigant: 2036+ patent timeline provides 10+ years of exclusivity. By then, next-generation GLP-1/GIP compounds will be the commercial product.

Risk Verdict
Risk Summary · LLY · ELEVATED RISK · 1 Critical · 3 High Risk Factors
Lilly's risk profile is moderate to elevated. The IRA drug pricing risk is real and probably inevitable — the question is magnitude, not whether it occurs. The Novo Nordisk competition is a permanent feature of the GLP-1 landscape but the SURMOUNT-5 superiority data provides durable clinical differentiation. Manufacturing constraints are the most immediate near-term risk to FY2026 revenue guidance. Most dangerous risk: IRA negotiated price cut of 40%+ on tirzepatide combined with manufacturing capacity miss in the same year.
