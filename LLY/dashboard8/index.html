Results Commentary
Lilly's Q4 2025 confirmed that tirzepatide is on a multi-year blockbuster trajectory. Combined Mounjaro ($3.88B) + Zepbound ($1.91B) revenue of $5.79B beat the $5.25B estimate by 10.3% — the largest quarterly beat in Lilly's GLP-1 history. Full-year 2025 tirzepatide revenue exceeded $15B in its second full commercial year, establishing one of the fastest pharmaceutical product ramps in history. Manufacturing capacity is gradually improving, though supply constraints remain the primary revenue governor.
Tirzepatide continues to demonstrate its value across diabetes, obesity, sleep apnea, and cardiovascular outcomes. We are committed to making these medicines available to as many patients as possible.
— David Ricks, CEO, Q4 2025 Earnings Call

Key Metrics Analysis
The 10.3% combined tirzepatide beat vs estimates is the defining metric — it proves demand continues to exceed supply, and that as manufacturing capacity comes online, revenue will grow proportionally. Non-GAAP gross margin of 83.1% (+60bps) confirms that GLP-1 pricing power remains intact despite IRA discussions. Kisunla (Alzheimer's) was the only miss at $110M vs $120M expected — early commercial adoption is slower due to ARIA monitoring requirements for infusion.
