Why Institutions Buy
B1
Only Tirzepatide Molecules — Patent Fortress to 2036
MOAT
Lilly's tirzepatide (Mounjaro/Zepbound) GLP-1/GIP dual agonist is clinically superior to semaglutide by the SURMOUNT-5 trial data. The tirzepatide molecule is patent-protected to approximately 2036 — giving Lilly a 10+ year window of exclusivity in the most exciting pharmaceutical market in history. Institutional pharma investors view 10-year exclusive access to a $200B market as the definition of a moat.
B2
EPS Growing from $22 → $42+ in 3 Years — Rare for a $720B Company
GROWTH
Lilly's EPS is expected to grow from $22 (FY2025) to $42+ (FY2028) — an 86% increase in 3 years for a company with a $720B market cap. This combination of size and growth rate is almost unprecedented in pharmaceutical history. Long-duration growth investors find the compounding EPS trajectory irresistible at the current 30x P/E (well below Lilly's 5-year average of 45x).
B3
3% GLP-1 Penetration of Eligible Patients — 20-Year Secular Growth
RUNWAY
Only 2-3% of eligible obese/diabetic patients currently use GLP-1 medications — the most compelling secular growth runway in pharmaceutical history. 880M obese adults globally, 40% US adult obesity rate, and expanding Medicare coverage create a demand pool that will not be exhausted in our investment horizon. Institutional healthcare investors view this as the closest analog to statins in the 1990s.
B4
Oral GLP-1 Removes Injection Barrier — Market Expansion 5-10x
NEW PRODUCTS
Lilly's oral GLP-1 (orforglipron) removes the injection barrier that limits current adoption — opening the 95-97% of eligible patients not currently treated. An oral daily pill at lower cost than weekly injections could expand the addressable GLP-1 market 5-10x from current levels. Institutional biotech specialists view the oral GLP-1 as the most transformational upcoming pharma launch.
Why Institutions Avoid
A1
IRA Medicare Drug Price Negotiation — 25-60% Price Risk
REGULATORY
The IRA empowers CMS to negotiate tirzepatide prices for Medicare patients — with prior negotiations achieving 25-79% discounts. A 40% tirzepatide price reduction for Medicare patients would impair peak revenue estimates by $5-8B annually. IRA-sensitive healthcare investors maintain reduced positions until the negotiation timeline and magnitude are known.
A2
Manufacturing Bottleneck — Supply Constrained Revenue
EXECUTION RISK
GLP-1 supply shortages have repeatedly impaired Lilly's ability to fulfill demand at full revenue potential. The $23B manufacturing investment is underway but takes 2-4 years from investment to production. Any manufacturing delay extends the period where demand exceeds supply, limiting revenue growth below the addressable market opportunity.

Investment Thesis
Long-Term Investment Thesis · LLY · Portfolio Manager Perspective
Eli Lilly is the closest thing to a 'sure thing' in growth investing — a 10-year patent fortress on the most explosive pharmaceutical market in history. The GLP-1 market is growing from $58B to $200B+ by 2033 at 23% CAGR, and tirzepatide's clinical superiority (SURMOUNT-5) ensures Lilly captures 45-55%+ of that market. The EPS growth trajectory ($22→$42 in 3 years) on a $720B company is almost unprecedented. Institutional healthcare allocators view Lilly as a mandatory core holding — not owning it constitutes unacceptable tracking error relative to the sector. The IRA pricing risk is real but ultimately manageable — only Medicare Part D patients face negotiated prices, not the commercially insured population. For long-duration investors, Lilly represents 15-20 years of GLP-1 secular growth anchored by patent protection — one of the most compelling compounders in large-cap healthcare history.
