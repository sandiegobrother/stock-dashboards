The EUV Monopoly — Why It Cannot Be Replicated
Technical Barriers

EUV lithography requires the convergence of five separately impossible engineering feats:

1. Light Source: 50,000 tin droplets/second hit by a CO₂ laser at 100kHz to generate 13.5nm EUV light. Cymer (ASML subsidiary) is the only commercial EUV light source manufacturer.

2. Optics: Zeiss SMT (ASML-partnered) manufactures mirrors polished to atomic smoothness — surface roughness below 0.1nm RMS. No other optics company produces EUV-grade mirrors at production scale.

3. Reticle Stage: The mask moves at 3m/s with sub-nanometer positioning accuracy in a vacuum. Stage control requires custom electromagnetic linear motors that took 15 years to perfect.

4. Wafer Stage: 450kg of precision mechanics operating in near-perfect vacuum, maintaining 0.1nm accuracy at manufacturing speeds.

5. System Integration: 100,000+ components from 800+ suppliers across 40+ countries, assembled in ASML's Veldhoven cleanrooms. EUV system assembly takes 1,000+ person-years per machine.

High-NA EUV — The Next Moat Layer
EXE:5200B High-NA System

ASML's High-NA EUV system (EXE:5200B) costs ~€400M per unit — the most expensive commercial instrument ever manufactured. It achieves 0.55 numerical aperture vs 0.33 for standard EUV, enabling chip features below 2nm.

Current customers in deployment: SK Hynix (HBM4 DRAM for AI accelerators), Samsung (advanced logic), Intel (18A / 14A roadmap). TSMC has not formally committed but its 1.4nm node timeline (2028) almost certainly requires High-NA deployment.

Revenue per High-NA system: ~€400M hardware + decades of IBM service at €30-50M/yr. Each new High-NA customer creates a 10-20 year recurring revenue relationship.

Moat Verdict · ASML · Ultra-Wide · Natural Monopoly · 9.7/10

ASML possesses the rarest type of competitive moat: a natural technological monopoly in a market of irreplaceable strategic necessity. Every semiconductor company making chips below 7nm — including every AI GPU, HBM memory chip, and smartphone processor — must use ASML's EUV systems. There is no alternative, and there will not be one for at least a decade. The High-NA EUV platform extends this monopoly another 10-15 years beyond current EUV. The moat is not just wide — it is compounding. By the time a competitor reaches first-generation EUV capability, ASML will be 2-3 generations ahead on High-NA and Hyper-NA. The only legitimate moat risk is a geopolitical event forcing full China self-sufficiency — even then, China would lose access to AI chips requiring EUV, not gain EUV capability.
