China Risk Deep Dive
China Revenue Exposure
China Risk Assessment

China revenue peaked at ~42% of ASML's total in H2 2024 as Chinese chipmakers front-loaded DUV purchases before export controls tightened. This elevated base creates a significant 2026 comparison headwind.

Management has guided China to "decline significantly" in 2026 — estimated €5-8B revenue reduction vs 2024-2025 levels. However:

The replacement math works: TSMC's Taiwan revenue grew +175% YoY in 2025. South Korea +46%. US +11%. Japan +50%. These represent ~60% of ASML's global revenue replacing the 30-40% China decline within 12-24 months.

Structural protection: China's front-loading means their installed base needs service — IBM revenue from China is partially protected even if new system sales decline. China cannot switch to a non-ASML service provider for their existing ASML machines.

Risk Verdict · ASML · Medium-Low · China Is the Only Material Near-Term Risk

ASML's risk profile is MEDIUM-LOW despite geopolitical headlines. The financial foundation (€13.3B cash, zero net debt, €11B FCF) eliminates financial risk. The technological monopoly eliminates competitive risk. The €38.8B backlog eliminates near-term revenue risk. The China export risk is real but partially self-healing — non-China capex is growing fast enough to replace China within 2 years, and the IBM revenue tail from existing China installations provides a buffer. The truly catastrophic tail risk (full Taiwan conflict) is low probability and is a risk for every global technology investment, not unique to ASML.
