Multiple Justification

ASML's 38x forward P/E trades at a 40-70% premium to semiconductor equipment peers. This premium is justified by:

1. Monopoly pricing power: ASML can raise prices 10-15% annually on EUV systems because customers have no alternative. This is impossible for Applied Materials or Lam Research, which compete with each other and with TEL.

2. IBM recurring revenue (25% and growing): Service/upgrade revenue at 60%+ margins creates a compounding annuity stream. KLA's inspection monopoly commands 28x P/E — ASML's EUV monopoly is stronger, justifying the premium.

3. High-NA system ASP escalation: €400M per High-NA system vs €200M for standard EUV means ASP is doubling while unit count grows. Revenue per installed system is compounding.

Bull / Base / Bear Scenarios
Bear Case — $1,050

Triggers: China revenue -50% (export control escalation), semiconductor capex recession in 2026, High-NA ramp 12-month delay.

FY2026 revenue misses guide at €32B. P/E compresses to 32x. EPS €28 → stock €896 / $1,050.

Base Case — $1,600

Assumes: FY2026 revenue €36B (mid-guide), China replacement by TSMC/Samsung/Micron, High-NA ramps as planned. P/E holds 38x on €30 EPS → €1,140 / $1,340 FY2026 + multiple expansion to $1,600 by year-end.

Bull Case — $2,000

Triggers: FY2026 revenue €39B+ (high-end guide hit), TSMC commits to High-NA accelerated timeline, memory capex above consensus. P/E re-rates to 42x on €34 EPS → $1,700+. Bernstein target $1,911 supports this range.

Valuation Verdict · ASML · Base Case $1,600 · ~10% Upside from Current

ASML is fairly valued to modestly undervalued at current prices (~$1,461). The stock is not cheap on traditional metrics (38x P/E), but traditional metrics don't capture the monopoly optionality of High-NA EUV. Each new High-NA customer generates €400M upfront + decades of high-margin service — Bernstein's $1,911 target reflects this optionality. Our base case of $1,600 is conservative; the bull case of $2,000 is achievable if TSMC formally commits to High-NA in its 1.4nm roadmap.
