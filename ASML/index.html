Company Overview
Business Model

ASML Holding N.V., headquartered in Veldhoven, Netherlands, is the world's sole manufacturer of Extreme Ultraviolet (EUV) lithography systems — the machines required to manufacture the world's most advanced semiconductor chips below 7nm. Founded in 1984 as a joint venture between Philips and ASMI, ASML has spent 30+ years building an unassailable technological moat.

Revenue comes from two streams: system sales (EUV, DUV, and metrology tools to TSMC, Samsung, Intel, Micron, SK Hynix) and Installed Base Management (IBM) — recurring service, upgrades, and spare parts that now represent €8.2B (25%+) of annual revenue and growing.

ASML's EUV machines cost €200-400M each. The High-NA EUV system (EXE:5200B) — the next-generation machine required for sub-2nm chips — costs approximately €400M per unit and has no competitor on the planet. This is not merely market leadership; it is a natural monopoly embedded in the global technology supply chain.

Investment Thesis Summary

ASML is the single most critical chokepoint in global semiconductor manufacturing. Every advanced chip — every AI GPU, every smartphone processor, every HBM memory chip — flows through ASML's lithography systems.

The AI infrastructure supercycle is driving unprecedented demand: TSMC's $165B+ capex plan through 2028, Samsung's HBM4 ramp, Micron's AI memory expansion, and Intel's recovery plan all depend on ASML's systems. Record Q4 2025 bookings of €13.2B (vs €6.9B expected, +91% beat) is the most powerful order intake signal in company history.

FY2026 guidance of €34-39B revenue (+4-19% YoY) and a new €12B share buyback program through 2028 underpin the investment case. The 30-year technological lead in EUV means no credible competitor exists — China's domestic alternatives remain 10-15 years behind on system integration.

Competitive Moat
Why ASML Has No Competitor

EUV technology required 30 years and $10B+ in R&D to develop. The EUV light source operates at 13.5nm wavelength — generated by firing 50,000 droplets of tin per second with a CO₂ laser. The optical system requires mirrors polished to atomic smoothness. ASML controls the entire supply chain through equity stakes in ZEISS (optics), Trumpf (laser), and Cymer (light source).

Nikon and Canon — ASML's only nominal competitors — manufacture DUV (deep ultraviolet) systems for mature nodes but have explicitly abandoned EUV development. There is no EUV competitor in production, in pilot, or credibly in development anywhere outside ASML's facilities.

China's SMEE (Shanghai Micro Electronics Equipment) produces i-line and KrF systems roughly equivalent to ASML's late-1990s technology. Chinese semiconductor executives themselves estimate a 10-15 year gap to EUV system capability — after which ASML would be shipping High-NA and Hyper-NA.

China Revenue Normalization

China represented approximately 30-42% of ASML revenue in 2024-2025 as Chinese chipmakers front-loaded DUV purchases before export control tightening. Management has explicitly guided that China revenue will "decline significantly" in 2026.

The bear case: China normalization removes €5-8B in annual revenue, creating a 15-25% headwind. The bull case: non-China capex from TSMC (+175% YoY revenue), South Korea (+46%), and US (+11%) is growing fast enough to replace China within 2 years. The €13.2B Q4 2025 bookings (91% above the €6.9B estimate) — of which 56% was from memory customers (Korea/US-centric) — directly validates the replacement thesis.

Investment Verdict
BUY · Price Target $1,600 · 12-Month Upside ~19% · March 2026

ASML is the most strategically irreplaceable company in the global semiconductor supply chain. Every advanced AI chip, every HBM memory module, every smartphone SoC depends on ASML's lithography systems — there is no alternative supplier, there will not be one for at least a decade, and the AI infrastructure supercycle is accelerating demand across all customer categories simultaneously.

The FY2025 results (€32.7B revenue, €9.6B net income, €11B FCF, €38.8B backlog) confirm operational excellence. The record Q4 2025 bookings of €13.2B — 91% above analyst expectations — is the most powerful forward indicator available: it implies FY2026 revenue at the high end of €34-39B guidance and potentially above. The €12B buyback through 2028 on a €590B market cap (~2% annual share reduction) adds meaningful per-share accretion.

At ~35x forward P/E for the world's only EUV monopoly with 52%+ gross margins and €11B annual FCF, the valuation is a premium but not excessive — Bernstein targets $1,911, RBC targets $1,625, UBS targets $1,400. Our base case of $1,600 implies 19% upside. Bull case $2,000 (10-15% above Bernstein) if High-NA ramps faster than expected and China replacement accelerates.
