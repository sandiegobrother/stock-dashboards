Why Institutions Buy ASML
B1
Mandatory Global Technology Holding — No Alternative Exists
MONOPOLY
For any institutional fund with technology sector exposure, NOT owning ASML is an active decision to exclude the only company that manufactures the machines that manufacture all advanced chips. Every AI infrastructure fund, every semiconductor fund, every global technology fund includes ASML as a core holding. The 0.9% short interest (vs 2-5% for most tech companies) reflects the near-universal institutional consensus that ASML is a long-only position.
B2
€38.8B Backlog — Highest Revenue Visibility in Equipment Sector
VISIBILITY
The €38.8B backlog (record Q4 2025 bookings of €13.2B — 91% above the €6.9B estimate) gives institutional investors something rare in cyclical industries: 14+ months of contracted revenue certainty. Long-duration investors (pension funds, endowments) find ASML's backlog-driven revenue predictability uniquely valuable. The Q4 bookings beat also implies FY2027 revenue significantly above prior consensus.
B3
€12B Buyback + Dividend — Best-in-Class Capital Return
CAPITAL RETURN
€12B buyback program through 2028 on a ~€590B market cap represents ~2% annual share count reduction. Combined with the 2.4% dividend yield, total shareholder return from capital returns alone reaches ~4.4% annually before stock appreciation. For dividend-mandated institutional allocators (sovereign wealth funds, insurance companies), ASML's growing dividend track record and €11B FCF generation make it one of the highest-quality industrial/technology dividend growers globally.
Why Institutions Avoid / Reduce
A1
38x P/E — Premium Multiple Limits Value-Mandated Buyers
VALUATION
At 38x forward P/E, ASML trades at a significant premium to industrial and even semiconductor equipment peers. Value-mandated funds (P/E < 20x screens), dividend value funds, and deep-value hedge funds screen ASML out entirely. Traditional semiconductor equipment comparables (Applied Materials ~22x, Lam Research ~24x) make ASML appear 50-75% expensive on a pure P/E basis. This creates a permanent institutional buyer gap at the value end of the spectrum.
A2
Geopolitical Risk — Netherlands/Taiwan/China Nexus
GEOPOLITICAL
ASML sits at the geopolitical intersection of US-China technology competition, Taiwan sovereignty, and Dutch strategic asset policy. Geopolitically-constrained funds (some sovereign wealth funds, ESG-flagged China-exposure policies, institutional risk limits on Taiwan-concentrated supply chains) maintain underweight positions. The Dutch government can theoretically restrict exports — this is an unmodeled tail risk that some risk management frameworks price conservatively.

Investment Thesis
Long-Term Investment Thesis · ASML · Portfolio Manager Perspective

ASML is the most competitively entrenched industrial company in the world — a natural monopoly embedded in the global technology supply chain that cannot be disrupted in any realistic scenario over a 10-year investment horizon. As a hedge fund PM, this is a core holding that requires an active decision to exclude, not include.

The structural case: every advanced semiconductor — every AI GPU, every HBM memory chip, every smartphone processor — requires ASML's EUV systems. TSMC's $165B capex plan, Samsung's HBM4 ramp, and Micron's AI memory expansion all flow through ASML's order book. The €38.8B backlog (+€13.2B record Q4 bookings) provides unprecedented revenue certainty for a capital equipment company.

The High-NA transition is the step-change catalyst: ASP doubling from €200M to €400M per system, combined with 50-80 annual units by 2027, creates a compounding revenue engine that the current 38x P/E does not fully price. The €12B buyback through 2028 (2% annual float reduction) adds per-share accretion. For a 3-5 year horizon, ASML at current prices is an opportunity, not a risk.

Final Institutional Verdict · ASML · STRONG BUY
STRONG BUY — EUV Monopoly Is a Once-in-a-Generation Franchise. ASML at ~$1,461 with €38.8B backlog, record Q4 bookings of €13.2B, and High-NA ASP doubling is modestly undervalued relative to the 10-year earnings power. Bernstein's $1,911 target (+31%) reflects the High-NA optionality. Our base case of $1,600 (+9.5%) is conservative. Recommended allocation: 3-5% of global technology portfolio.
