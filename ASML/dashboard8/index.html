Results Commentary

ASML's FY2025 results were a comprehensive beat driven by EUV system volume acceleration and record-breaking Q4 bookings. Full-year revenue of €32.7B (+15.6% YoY) beat the €31.5B estimate by 3.8%. FY2025 net income of €9.6B (+26.9% YoY) and EPS of €24.73 (+28.5%) beat estimates by 7.5%, reflecting gross margin expansion to 52.8% (+150bps from FY2024) and operating leverage. EUV system sales reached €11.6B from 48 systems shipped (+39% YoY by units), while High-NA EUV moved from pilot to active customer deployment.

The defining metric of Q4 2025 was not revenue — it was bookings: €13.2B in net orders (vs €6.9B expected, +91% beat) is one of the largest single-quarter booking beats in semiconductor equipment history. Of this, 56% came from memory customers (Samsung, SK Hynix, Micron) — confirming AI-driven memory capex is accelerating sharply into 2026-2027.

"Our Q4 bookings of €13.2 billion demonstrate the strength of the demand environment. AI is driving unprecedented investment in both logic and memory capacity — and ASML is uniquely positioned to benefit from every dollar spent on advanced semiconductor manufacturing."
— Christophe Fouquet, CEO, Q4 2025 Earnings Call, January 28, 2026

Guidance Analysis

The FY2026 guidance of €34-39B (wide range) was the most discussed element of the call. The midpoint of €36.5B vs €33B consensus represents a 10.6% guidance beat — but the wide range (±7%) reflects genuine uncertainty about China revenue normalization pace and High-NA ramp speed. Bulls focused on the €39B ceiling (achievable if TSMC commits to High-NA and China normalization is gradual). Bears noted the €34B floor represents only +4% growth. The €12B buyback authorization — not expected by analysts — was the most positive capital allocation signal, implying management confidence in FCF durability at the high end of guidance.

The +4.3% after-hours move reflected the dual positive catalysts of the record €13.2B Q4 bookings and the €12B buyback announcement. Analyst upgrades followed immediately: UBS raised from €1,030 to €1,400 (~+36%), Bernstein raised from $1,642 to $1,911 (+16%), RBC raised from $1,550 to $1,625. The revenue beat (+3.8%) was secondary to the bookings beat (+91%) — the latter directly signals FY2026 and FY2027 revenue substantially above prior estimates. The wide FY2026 guidance range (€34-39B) limited the stock's initial move as China normalization uncertainty persisted.
