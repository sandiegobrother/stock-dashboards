P/E Assessment
Netflix at 35x forward P/E is above the entertainment average of 25x but below its historical 5-year average of 45x. The de-rating from 50x (2024) to 35x (2025) reflects EPS growth catching up to the stock price. At $27.50 in FY2025 EPS and growing 20%+, the PEG ratio is approximately 1.7x — reasonable for the only streaming platform at 301M subscribers with NFL + WWE live sports exclusivity. The path to 35%+ operating margins from advertising revenue scaling would drive further EPS growth and multiple expansion.

DCF Analysis
The base DCF of $920 is slightly below current $960, suggesting Netflix is approximately fairly valued. The bull case $1,300 requires ad revenue materially accelerating FCF beyond $10B — achievable if the advertising tier reaches $5-6B in 2026-2027. The bear case $700 requires content cost inflation exceeding revenue growth, which is possible but manageable given Netflix's $17B annual spend discipline. The optionality from gaming, live events, and international market expansion provides additional upside not fully captured in the base case.
