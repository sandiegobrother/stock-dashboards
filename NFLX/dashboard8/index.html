Results Commentary
Netflix's Q4 2025 was one of its best subscriber quarters ever — 19M net adds vs 9.5M expected was the biggest positive surprise of the earnings report. The ad-supported tier reached 40M+ MAU (14% above estimates), confirming the ad business is scaling faster than expected. The password sharing crackdown's impact has been fully absorbed and the growth engine is genuinely driven by new subscriber acquisition. EPS of $4.27 (+22%) beat driven by operating leverage. Total subscribers reached 301M — first time above 300M.
Our advertising business continues to scale at a rapid pace, reaching over 40 million monthly active users in the ad tier. 2026 will be the year we reach critical mass for advertising.
— Greg Peters, Co-CEO, Q4 2025 Earnings Call

Key Metrics Analysis
The 19M net subscriber adds vs 9.5M expected was the single biggest beat in Netflix history — 2x the estimate. This was driven by NFL Christmas games (24M+ viewers) that converted trial users to paid subscribers, combined with Squid Game Season 2 global engagement. Ad-supported MAU of 40M (above 35M estimate) confirms the advertising business is on track for $3-5B+ in revenue by 2026-2027. ARPU was slightly below expectations at $17.10 — the ad-tier mix shift is diluting average revenue per subscriber, which is expected and acceptable given the volume.

Guidance Analysis
The Q1 2026 revenue guide of $10.42B vs $10.28B consensus is steady but not dramatic — Netflix tends to guide conservatively. The 28.2% Q1 operating margin guide (+30bps above consensus) confirms the margin expansion narrative. The FY2026 ~29% operating margin target vs 22.2% in Q4 (seasonality) reflects the full-year discipline. Netflix did not announce a major content spend increase for 2026 — cost discipline is being maintained even as advertising revenue scales.
