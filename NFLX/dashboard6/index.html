Key Growth Drivers
01
Advertising Tier — $5-8B Revenue by 2027E
Market Expansion
VERY HIGH
Netflix's ad-supported tier reached 40M+ monthly active users in 2025 — and advertising CPMs for streaming are 2-3x higher than traditional TV. With 40M ad-supported subscribers growing to 80M+ by 2027, advertising revenue could reach $5-8B annually, representing an entirely new, high-margin revenue stream layered on top of subscription growth.

Live Sports — NFL, WWE, F1 Exclusive
New Products
HIGH
Netflix's NFL Christmas games (2025: 24M+ viewers), WWE Raw (exclusive from Jan 2025), and F1 documentary/rights create appointment viewing that drives subscriptions and ad pricing premium. Live sports is the most defensible content category — it cannot be pirated or time-shifted. Sports rights are transitioning to streaming from linear TV.

Industry Growth Context
The global streaming market is growing at 12% CAGR — but Netflix is growing faster by adding advertising revenue on top of subscription growth. The combination of 301M subscribers + advertising + live sports + gaming creates a 4-pillar revenue model that is more defensible than any single-revenue streaming platform. Linear TV's accelerating decline ($30B+ in TV advertising shifting to streaming annually) is a structural tailwind that benefits Netflix most given its scale.

Growth Verdict
Growth Potential Verdict · NFLX · +11% → +13% Est. Revenue Growth 2-4 Years
Netflix's growth potential is steady and compounding. Revenue growing from $43B to $62B+ over 2025-2028 (44% cumulative) may appear modest, but the earnings growth (EPS $22 → $40+, +80%) is more impressive due to operating leverage. The advertising tier is the biggest near-term upside — if 80M+ ad-supported subscribers generate $5-8B in high-margin advertising by 2027, it would represent 25-35% incremental EPS growth not currently in consensus estimates.
